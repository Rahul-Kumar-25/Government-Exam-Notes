Basic - SUN

SUN

  • Hydrogen, Helium gas present
  • Nuclear fusion happens in Sun
  • Reacts as Nuclear Reactor

H : He = 3 : 1

  • During nuclear fusion hydrogen transforms into helium
  • Incoming solar radiation are called Insolation

(Diagram: Insolation and Albedo – Sun–Earth)

Tropic of Cancer and Capricorn

  • Maximum insolation throughout the year
  • 23.5° till 66° = max insolation (summer)
  • Solstice 21 June – Northern Hemisphere (max insolation)
  • Winter solstice – 22 Dec – Southern Hemisphere
  • Minimum insolation – Autumn Equinox – 23 September (Fall)
  • Minimum insolation – Spring Equinox – 21 March (Vernal)
  • 66° till 90° → Max and min insolation throughout the year
  • Midnight Sun phenomenon also known as Polar Day (24 hrs day)
  • Earth absorbs some solar radiation and reflects back some (Albedo)
  • This reflected radiation is also known as Terrestrial Radiation
  • Snow → High Albedo
  • Water → Low Albedo
  • Forest → Lowest Albedo
  • Sun → Earth are of shorter wavelength
    • UV
    • Visible light

(Diagram: Heat zones of Earth)

  • Earth → Sun are of longer wavelength
    • Thermal rays
    • Infrared
  • Perihelion:
    • Planets and comets near to Sun
    • When Earth closest to Sun
    • Jan (2 to 6)
  • Aphelion:
    • Planets and comets far from Sun
    • 4th July
    • When Earth is farthest from Sun

Earth revolves around the Sun not in circular path but in ellipse given by Johannes Kepler

Orbit Around Sun

  • Rock planet → M V E Mars (Terrestrial planet)
  • Jovian / Gaseous planet → J S U N
  • Nearest star from Earth = Sun
  • Syzygy – when three planets or stars are in a straight line

Eclipses

  • Sun – Moon – Earth → Solar Eclipse
  • Sun – Earth – Moon → Lunar Eclipse
  • Solar day = 24 hrs (considering Sun)
  • 23 hrs 56 min 4 sec = Sidereal day (with respect to fixed stars)
  • Speed of Earth = 1667 km/hr (at its axis)
  • Shape of Earth = Oblate spheroid
  • One year = 365 days 5 hr 48 min 45 sec
