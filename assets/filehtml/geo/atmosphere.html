Geography

Basic - Atmosphere

Atmosphere

Atmosphere: Thick gases envelope around the earth.

Layers

Troposphere

  • 0–13 km
  • Thickness:
    • At equator up to 18 km
    • At poles 8 km approx
  • Weather phenomena occur in this layer only
  • Temperature decreases with height
  • 90% of gases are present here
  • Tropopause: –58°C
    • Also known as zone of constant temperature
    • Also called isothermal zone

Normal Lapse Rate

  • Every 165 meters = 1°C decrease
  • Or 6.5°C per 1 km

Stratosphere (50 km)

  • It has ozone layer
  • More suitable, no disturbance for:
    • Plane
    • Jet aircraft
    • Weather balloon
  • No disturbance due to presence of less gases
  • This layer is devoid of major weather phenomena
  • Due to ozone and radiation, temperature increases
  • Stratopause: at 4°C (steady)

Mesosphere (80 km)

  • With height temperature decreases up to –90°C
  • Meteors fall and burn here only
  • Federation Aeronautique Internationale
  • Karman line: 100 km

Thermosphere (85–600 km / 85–513 km)

  • Also known as upper atmosphere
  • Contains electrically charged particles (ions)
  • Hence thermosphere contains ionosphere
  • Ionosphere helps in reflecting radio waves transmitted by earth
  • Helps in telecommunication
  • High temperature breaks gas molecules creating ions
  • Temperature increases with height (up to ~1200°C)
  • Thermopause
  • International Space Station (ISS)
  • Satellite orbits
  • Aurora Borealis = North Pole / Northern Hemisphere
  • Aurora Australis = South Pole / Southern Hemisphere

Auroras

  • Auroras are natural display of light in sky at high altitude (Arctic and Antarctic)
  • Charged particles of sun interact with earth’s magnetic field
  • Collision induces energy in light form
  • Colour of aurora depends on:
    • Gas involved
    • Altitude of collision

Exosphere (up to 1000 km)

  • Karman line = 100 km
  • Beyond 100 km → space
  • Under 100 km → territory
  • 1000 km separates astronauts
