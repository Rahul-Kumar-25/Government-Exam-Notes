Tsunami (Government Exam Oriented Notes)

What is Tsunami?

Tsunami is a series of high sea waves caused by:

  • Undersea earthquake (most common)
  • Volcanic eruption
  • Submarine landslide
  • Meteorite impact

Meaning of Tsunami

  • Tsu = Harbour
  • Nami = Wave

Tsunami means: Harbour Wave

2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami (Very Important)

  • Date: 26 December 2004
  • Epicenter: Near Sumatra, Indonesia
  • Magnitude: 9.1 – 9.3

Affected Indian States

  • Tamil Nadu (most affected)
  • Andhra Pradesh
  • Kerala
  • Andaman & Nicobar Islands

Deaths: Approximately 2.3 lakh people (multi-country)

Tsunami vs Normal Sea Waves

  • Tsunami waves have long wavelength and high energy
  • Speed in deep ocean: 700–800 km/hr
  • Wave height increases near coast due to shallow water

Warning Signs of Tsunami

  • Sudden withdrawal of sea water
  • Strong coastal earthquake
  • Loud roaring sound from ocean

Tsunami Warning System in India

  • INCOIS – Hyderabad
  • Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services
  • Works under Ministry of Earth Sciences

Uses

  • Seismographs
  • Tsunameters
  • Satellite data

Difference: Earthquake vs Tsunami

  • Earthquake: Shaking of earth’s crust
  • Tsunami: Sea waves generated due to undersea disturbance
