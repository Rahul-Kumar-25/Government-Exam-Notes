Weather & Climate

Weather Climate
  • Description of weather condition of a particular place at a particular time.
  • Covers short period of time.
  • Study of weather: Meteorology.
  • Composite / integrated picture of weather condition over a longer period of time.
  • Average atmospheric condition of an area over considerable time (min 35 years).
  • Study of climate: Climatology.

Elements of Weather & Climate

1. Rainfall

  • Also include other forms of precipitation like snow, sleet & hail.
  • Measured by Raingauge.

2. Pressure

  • Measured by Barometer.
  • Aneroid Barometer
  • Altimeter
  • Barograph
  • Micro barometer
  • Micro barograph

3. Temperature

  • Measured by Thermometer (Mercury present).

4. Humidity

  • Measure of dampness of atmosphere.
Absolute Humidity
  • Total weight of moisture content per volume of air at a definite temperature.
  • Unit: gram/m³.
Specific Humidity
  • Mass of water vapour in grams contained in a kilogram of air.
  • Actual quantity of moisture present in definite air.
Relative Humidity
  • Ratio of actual amount of water vapour to total amount of air at a given temperature.
  • When R.H is 100%, it is called dew point.
  • Further cooling → water vapour → condensation → cloud form / rain.
  • R.H ↑ means air is moist (equatorial region).
  • R.H ↓ means air is dry (deserts).
  • Instrument: Hygrometer.

5. Wind

  • Air in motion that has both speed and direction.
  • Instrument: Wind vane, wind clock.
  • Speed of wind measured by Anemometer.
  • Wind measured by Beaufort wind scale.

6. Sunshine

  • Amount of sunshine received depends on latitude.
  • Position of earth in revolution around the sun.

7. Clouds

  • R.H when hits the dew point → condensation → rain / snow.
  • Heavy ones fall during condensation.
  • Lighter particles remain suspended in air and float as cloud.
  • Classification based on form, height, appearance.

4 Types of Clouds

Type Details
High Clouds (Cirrus)
  • 20,000 – 40,000 feet or 6–20 km.
  • Cirro-cumulus
  • Cirro-stratus
Medium Clouds (Alto)
  • 7,000 – 20,000 feet or 2–7 km.
  • Alto-cumulus
  • Alto-stratus
Low Clouds (Stratus)
  • Below 7,000 feet or below 2 km.
  • Strato-cumulus
  • Nimbo-stratus (Rain)
Cumulus Clouds
  • 2,000 – 13,000 feet.
  • Great vertical extent.
  • Cumulonimbus (Thunder and Rain).

Jet Streams

  • Winds blowing near the tropopause.
  • 150 km wide, 2–3 km thick.
  • Speed: 150–200 km/h, at core up to 395 km/h.

Types of Jet Streams

  • Polar Night Jet Stream – beyond 60° latitude.
  • Polar Frontal Jet Stream – 30°–70° North latitude, 9–12 km height, also known as Rossby waves.
  • Sub-tropical Westerly Jet Stream – 20°–35° North latitude, 10–14 km height, responsible for western disturbance in India (Dec–Feb).
  • Tropical Easterly Jet Stream – 25°–35° North, height 14–16 km, responsible for Indian Monsoon.

Wind

Surface Wind

  • Primary wind (Permanent / Planetary).
  • Secondary wind (Seasonal / Periodic).
  • Tertiary wind (Local winds).

Primary Wind

  • Also known as permanent & planetary wind.
  • Almost same direction throughout the year.

Secondary Wind

  • Also known as seasonal & periodic wind.
  • Example: Monsoon winds.

Tertiary Wind

  • Also known as local winds.
  • Examples: Sea breeze, land breeze, mountain & valley wind.

Mountain Wind

  • At night mountain slopes cool faster than valley.
  • Air over slope becomes denser and heavier.
  • Flows into the valley.
  • Also known as Katabatic wind.

Valley Wind

  • During the day sun heats mountain slopes faster than valley.
  • Creates low pressure on slope and high pressure in valley.
  • Air flows from valley to mountain.
  • Also known as Anabatic wind.
