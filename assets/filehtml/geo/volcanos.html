Volcanic Land Form

Extrusive volcanic land form

Name Features
Conical Vent Narrow cylindrical vent through which magma flows violently
Fissure Vent More common in basaltic volcanoes
Lava comes out through narrow and linear vents
(Generally no explosive activity)
Shield Volcano Bowl shaped with gentle slopes
Lava is less viscous
Covers large area
Example: Mauna Loa (Hawaii)
Composite Volcano Built up by layers of solidified lava
Violent eruption
Viscous lava
Example: Vesuvius, Fujiyama
Cinder Cone Volcano Steep circular hill made of loose pyroclastic fragments
Explosive
Erupt only once
Example: Karakatau (Indonesia)
Crater Volcano Inverted cone shaped vent
Appears as depression if crater is formed
Caldera Volcano Most explosive volcano
Collapse on themselves forming caldera
Example: Aso (Japan)
Flood Basalt Giant eruption or series of eruptions
Covers large surface
Example: Deccan Plateau (Maharashtra)
Mid Ocean Ridge Volcano Occurs in oceanic areas
Basaltic lava
Spreads on both sides of ridge
Responsible for sea floor spreading
