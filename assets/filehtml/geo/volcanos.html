Volcanic Land Form

Extrusive volcanic land form

Conical Vent

Narrow cylindrical vent through which magma flow violently.

Fissure Vent

  • More common in Basaltic Volcanoes.
  • Where lava come out through narrow and linear vents.
  • (general no explosive activity)

Shield Volcano

  • Bowl shaped with gentle slopes (lava is less viscous)
  • It covers a large area
  • Example – Mauna Loa (Hawaii)

Composite Volcano

  • Built up by layers of solidify lava
  • exp – Vesuvius, Fujiyama
  • (have violent eruption)
  • (viscous lava)
  • (less dense)

Cindercone Volcano

  • Steep circular hill structure made up of loose pyroclastic fragments (explosion)
  • erupt only once
  • exp – Karakatau (Indonesia)

Crater Volcano

  • Inverted cone shape vent through which magma flows out
  • (It appears as a depression if the volcano form craters)

Caldera Volcano

  • Most explosive volcanoes that tend to fall on themselves forming calderas rather than tall structure
  • exp – Aso (Japan)

Flood basalt

  • Formed due to giant eruption or series of eruptions that cover a large stretch of the surface
  • exp – Deccan Plateau (Maharashtra)

Mid Ocean Ridge Volcano

  • Occurs in oceanic areas
  • has basaltic lava
  • spread on both sides of ridge
  • It is responsible for sea floor spreading
