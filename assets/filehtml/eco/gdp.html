Macro Economices • GDP

GDP (Gross Domestic Product)

Total final value of goods & services produced within domestic territory of a country in specified time period.

Methods of Calculating GDP

1. Product or Value Added Method

Value added by a firm = value of final good − value of initial good

Example: Farmer producing wheat of ₹100 without any assistance

Farmer → ₹100
Wheat produced = ₹50 (consumption) + ₹50 (selling)

Baker → ₹200
Bread produced = ₹150 (value added) + ₹50 (bought)

Then total GDP:

Value added by Baker + Value added by Farmer
= ₹(200 − 50) + 100
= ₹250

Production Method

GDP → Final value of all goods produced

= Final value of wheat + Final value of bread
= ₹50 (consumed) + ₹200 (bread)

Only this is final good, other wheat is investment good

Total GDP = ₹250

2. Expenditure Method

Whatever goods and services are produced in the economy are ultimately purchased by the four sectors of the economy:

  • Household sector
  • Government sector
  • Private sector
  • External sector

If we add the expenditure done by these four sectors on final goods and services produced within the domestic territory, it will be equal to GDP.

  • Household sector spends on consumption (C')
  • Private sector spends only on capital goods (I')
  • Government sector purchases both capital & consumption goods (G')
  • External sector purchases both consumption and capital goods → Export (X')

GDP = C' + I' + G' + X'

C + I + G − (Cm + Im + Gm) + X
= C + I + G + X − M

OR

GDP = Private consumption + Private investment + Government investment + Government consumption + Export − Import

GDP = Total consumption + Total investment + Export − Import

Major Components of GDP

  • Private consumption → 60%
  • Government consumption → 11%
  • Total investment → 29%
  • Exports → −3%

Note:

When government and external sector are present, savings and investment may vary, but generally rise in savings leads to rise in investment.

Household can spend income on:

Consumption + Saving + Tax

GDP = C + S + T

C + I + G + X − M = C + S + T

(I − S) + (G − T) = M − X

When G = T = M = X = 0, then I = S

3. Income Method

Sum of final goods produced in the economy is equal to the income received by factors of production:

  • Wages
  • Rent
  • Interest
  • Profit

GDP = Profit earned by firms + Interest on capital + Rent for natural resources + Wages earned by labour

GDP of India includes:

  • All states’ gross domestic product
  • Central activities (railways, defence, highways, etc.)
  • Embassies located in other countries
  • Fishing vessels, oil & natural gas rigs operated in international waters

Concept of Domestic Territory

  • Political frontiers of a country including territorial waters
  • Ships & aircraft operated by residents between two or more countries
  • Fishing vessels, oil & gas rigs operated in international waters
  • Embassies, consulates and military establishments abroad

GDP calculation by NSO (National Statistical Office) is done by:

  • Value added method
  • Expenditure method

GDP includes contribution of foreign nationals working in India.

If output earned by Indian residents only (India or abroad) is calculated, it is called GNP (Gross National Product).

Income earned by NRIs is not part of GDP.
