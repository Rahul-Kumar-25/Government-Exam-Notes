Economics – Basic Concepts

Economy • Fundamentals

What is Economics?

Economics deals with the allocation of scarce resources to satisfy unlimited human wants. The central problem of any economy is scarcity.

Central Problem of Economy

  • Limited Resources
  • Unlimited Wants
  • Leads to conflict and choice

Branches of Economics

  • Micro Economics: Study of individual units (consumer, firm)
  • Macro Economics: Study of economy as a whole (government, GDP, inflation)

Micro Economics Focus

  • Consumer behaviour (maximum utility at lowest cost)
  • Producer behaviour (maximum profit)

Production Possibility Set (PPS)

Production Possibility Set refers to all possible combinations of goods and services that can be produced with given resources and technology.

  • Resources are fixed
  • Technology is constant
  • Efficiency is assumed

Production Possibility Curve (PPC)

PPC shows the maximum combinations of two goods that can be produced when all resources are fully and efficiently utilised.

  • Represents full employment of resources
  • Shows opportunity cost
  • Downward sloping curve

Opportunity Cost

Opportunity cost is the cost of the next best alternative that is sacrificed to obtain one more unit of a good.

Example:

  • Choosing a ₹6,00,000 job over ₹4,00,000 → Opportunity cost = ₹4,00,000

Factors of Production

  • Land: Natural resources (Rent)
  • Labour: Human effort – physical & mental (Wages)
  • Capital: Tools, machinery, technology (Interest)
  • Entrepreneurship: Risk-taking, innovation, management (Profit)
