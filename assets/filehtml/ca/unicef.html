UNICEF (United Nations Children’s Fund)

Current Affairs • UN Agency

Headquarters

New York, USA

Introduction

  • UNICEF originally stood for United Nations International Children’s Emergency Fund.
  • Created in 1946 by the UN General Assembly.
  • Formed to provide emergency aid to children and mothers affected by World War II.
  • Initially focused on Europe; mandate expanded globally in 1950.

Mandate

  • Advocate for children’s rights.
  • Fulfil basic needs of children.
  • Expand opportunities for children worldwide.

Guiding Convention

Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC)

Funding

  • Entirely voluntary contributions.
  • Governments, NGOs, private donors and corporations.
  • Fund-raising campaigns.

Establishment & Status

  • Established on 11 December 1946.
  • Became a permanent part of the UN in 1953.
  • Reports to ECOSOC.
  • Founded by the UN General Assembly.

Major Achievement

  • Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize (1965).

Nature of Organisation

  • UNICEF is a UN agency working under the UN General Assembly.
  • Not a specialised agency like WHO or UNESCO.
  • Humanitarian and development agency focused on children and mothers.

Main Objectives & Functions

  • Works in more than 190 countries.

1. Child Health & Nutrition

  • Immunisation programmes.
  • Polio eradication.
  • Malnutrition reduction.

2. Education

  • Universal primary education.
  • Learning materials.
  • Support for girl child education.

3. Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH)

  • Clean drinking water.
  • Toilets in schools.
  • Hygiene awareness.

4. Child Protection

  • Prevention of child labour, trafficking and exploitation.
  • Support for refugees and war-affected children.

5. Emergency Response

  • Relief during natural disasters.
  • Support during conflicts and epidemics.

Governing Structure

Executive Board

  • 36 members.
  • Elected by ECOSOC.

Executive Director

  • Appointed for a 5-year term by the UN Secretary-General.
  • Current Executive Director: Catherine Russell (2022–).

UNICEF and India

  • Started work in India in 1949.
  • Ministry of Women & Child Development is the nodal ministry.

Key UNICEF Programmes in India

  • Polio eradication.
  • POSHAN for nutrition.
  • Support during White Revolution.
  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao support.
  • WASH programmes in schools.
  • COVID-19 vaccine delivery assistance.

Reports Published by UNICEF

  • The State of the World’s Children.
  • Progress for Children.
  • Child Alert Report.
