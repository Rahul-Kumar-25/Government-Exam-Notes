Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

Introduction

Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) is an intergovernmental political, economic and security alliance.

Headquarters

  • Beijing, China

Origin

  • Established in 1996 as Shanghai Five
  • Original members:
    • China
    • Russia
    • Kazakhstan
    • Kyrgyzstan
    • Tajikistan

Expansion Timeline

  • 2001: Uzbekistan joined → renamed as SCO (Summit: Shanghai, China)
  • 2015: Decision to admit India & Pakistan
  • 2016: India & Pakistan signed Memorandum of Organisation
  • 2017: India & Pakistan officially joined (Summit: Kazakhstan)
  • 2023: Iran joined (Summit: Goa, India)
  • 2024: Belarus joined (Summit: Pakistan)

Members & Status

  • Current Members: 10
  • Observers: Afghanistan, Mongolia
  • Dialogue Partners: Armenia, Azerbaijan, Bahrain, Cambodia, Egypt, Turkey, Maldives, Myanmar, Nepal, Qatar, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia, Sri Lanka, UAE

Aim

  • Promote cooperation and mutual support in:
    • Trade
    • Investment
    • Energy
    • Transport
    • Security

Shanghai Spirit (Core Values)

  • Mutual trust
  • Mutual benefit
  • Equality
  • Consultation
  • Respect for cultural diversity
  • Common development

Official Languages

  • Chinese
  • Russian

Objectives

  1. Strengthen relations among member states
  2. Promote cooperation in political, economic, scientific, technical, cultural, tourism and transport fields
  3. Safeguard regional peace, security and stability
  4. Create a democratic and equitable international political and economic order

Organisational Structure

  1. Council of Heads of State – Highest decision-making body (Annual Summit)
  2. Council of Heads of Government – Second highest body (Annual Meeting)
  3. Council of Foreign Ministers
  4. Council of National Coordinators
  5. Secretariat – Beijing, China (Head: Secretary General)
  6. RATS – Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure (HQ: Tashkent, Uzbekistan)
  7. SCO Business Council
  8. SCO Interbank Consortium

Significance of SCO

  • Covers around 40% of world population
  • Accounts for nearly 20% of global GDP
  • Promotes regional stability and connectivity
  • Acts as a bulwark against terrorism and drug trafficking

Relevance for India

  • Counter-terrorism cooperation
  • Regional stability
  • Improved connectivity
  • Economic cooperation
  • Multilateral diplomacy
  • Stronger relations with Central Asia

Challenges for India

  • Managing relations with Pakistan
  • Ensuring economic benefits
  • Low bilateral trade with SCO countries
  • Balancing ties with China
