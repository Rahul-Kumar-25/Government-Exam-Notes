World Trade Organization (WTO)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

Introduction

World Trade Organization (WTO) is an intergovernmental organization that regulates and facilitates international trade between nations.

Headquarters

  • Geneva, Switzerland

Establishment

  • Established on 1 January 1995
  • Created after the Uruguay Round (1986–94)
  • Replaced GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade)

Evolution of WTO

  • GATT (1947)
    • Established in 1947
    • Came into operation on 1 January 1948
    • Trade only in goods
    • Temporary arrangement
  • Uruguay Round (1986–94)
    • Addressed services, intellectual property and dispute settlement
    • Led to creation of WTO
  • WTO Era
    • Formally established through Marrakesh Agreement (1994)
    • Agreement signed in Morocco
    • WTO came into force on 1 January 1995

Membership

  • Total Members: 166 (including India)
  • Original GATT members automatically became WTO members

Objectives

  • Promote free and fair trade
  • Reduce tariff and non-tariff barriers
  • Ensure predictability and transparency in trade
  • Raise standard of living and employment
  • Facilitate sustainable development
  • Resolve trade disputes peacefully

Functions of WTO

  • Administers and implements trade agreements
  • Acts as a forum for trade negotiations
  • Settles trade disputes through dispute settlement mechanism
  • Reviews national trade policies
  • Provides technical assistance to developing countries
  • Cooperates with IMF and World Bank

Principles of WTO

  • Most Favoured Nation (MFN) – equal treatment to all trading partners
  • National Treatment – imported goods treated like domestic goods
  • Free trade
  • Reduction of trade barriers
  • Fair competition
  • Transparency
  • Special and differential treatment for developing countries

Organisational Structure

  1. Ministerial Conference – Highest decision-making body (meets every 2 years)
  2. General Council – Day-to-day functioning body
  3. Dispute Settlement Body
  4. Trade Policy Review Body
  5. Councils under WTO
    • Council for Trade in Goods
    • Council for Trade in Services
    • Council for TRIPS

WTO Agreements

  • GATT (1994)
    • Deals with tariffs, quotas and subsidies
    • Based on MFN principle
  • GATS – General Agreement on Trade in Services
    • Banking, telecom, tourism, education
    • Four Modes of Supply
  • TRIPS – Trade Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights
    • Patents, copyrights, trademarks
    • Minimum patent protection: 20 years

Dispute Settlement Mechanism

  • Judicial system of WTO (quasi-judicial)
  • Steps:
    1. Consultation
    2. Panel Formation
    3. Appellate Body
  • WTO is known as the “Court of Global Trade”

India and WTO

  • India is a founding member
  • Major concerns:
    • Agricultural subsidies
    • Public stockholding for food security
    • TRIPS and access to medicines

Difference between GATT and WTO

  • GATT
    • Started in 1947
    • Trade only in goods
    • Temporary arrangement
    • Weak dispute system
  • WTO
    • Started in 1995
    • Trade in goods, services and intellectual property
    • Permanent organization
    • Strong dispute settlement system
