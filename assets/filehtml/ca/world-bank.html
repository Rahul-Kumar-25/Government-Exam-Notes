World Bank (WB)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

Headquarters

Washington, DC, USA

Introduction

  • International financial organization.
  • Established to reconstruct post–Second World War international economic system.
  • Later assists developing nations.
  • Founded in 1944 at the Bretton Woods Conference.
  • Officially began operations in June 1946.
  • Original name: IBRD (International Bank for Reconstruction and Development).

Membership

  • Founding members: 38
  • Current members: 189
  • India is a founding member.
  • IMF membership is compulsory to join the World Bank.
  • Membership in IDA, IFC and MIGA is conditional.

Important Facts

  • First loan given to France in 1947 for post-war reconstruction.
  • India’s first loan in 1949 for railway reconstruction.

Goals of World Bank

  1. Ending extreme poverty by 2030.
  2. Boosting shared prosperity of the poorest 40% population.

World Bank Group

  • IBRD – International Bank for Reconstruction and Development
  • IDA – International Development Association
  • IFC – International Finance Corporation
  • MIGA – Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency
  • ICSID – International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes

Establishment Years

  • IBRD – 1944
  • IDA – 1960
  • IFC – 1956
  • MIGA – 1988
  • ICSID – 1966

Purposes

  • IBRD: Financing for middle-income and credit-worthy developing countries. India is founding member.
  • IDA: Loans and grants to low-income developing countries.India is founding member.
  • IFC: Private sector investment in developing countries.India is founding member.
  • MIGA: Guarantees against non-commercial risks.India is founding member.
  • ICSID: Settlement of investment disputes.India is not a member.

IBRD + IDA = World Bank

IBRD + IDA + IFC + MIGA + ICSID = World Bank Group

Functions of World Bank

  1. Financing development projects.
  2. Providing advisory services.
  3. Data and research on global economic trends.
  4. Promoting private sector development.
  5. Supporting education and healthcare systems.
  6. Promoting environmental protection.

World Bank Structure

1. Board of Governors

  • Highest decision-making body.
  • One Governor from each member country.
  • Meets annually.

2. Board of Executive Directors

  • Handles daily operations.
  • Total 25 Executive Directors.
  • Decisions by majority voting.

3. President

  • Head of the institution.
  • Chairman of Executive Directors.
  • Traditionally an American citizen.
  • Current President: Ajay Banga.

4. Management & Staff

Economists, engineers, environmental specialists and development experts.

Sources of Funds

  1. Capital subscription from member countries.
  2. Borrowing from global capital markets.
  3. Loan repayments and interest income.
  4. Income from investments.

Voting Power

  • USA – 16.4%
  • Japan – 7.8%
  • Germany – 4.4%
  • UK – 4.3%
  • France – 4.3%
  • India – 2.9%

World Bank Projects in India

  • PM Gram Sadak yojna
  • National Ganga River Basin Project
  • Dam Rehabilitation and Improvement Project
  • Eastern Dedicated Freight Corridor
  • Bihar and kosi Flood Recovery Project
  • Grid-connected Rooftop Solar Programme
