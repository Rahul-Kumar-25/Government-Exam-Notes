World Health Organization (WHO)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

Headquarters

Geneva, Switzerland

Introduction

  • WHO is a specialised agency of the United Nations.
  • Established in 1948.
  • Leads global health efforts and sets international health standards.
  • World Health Day is observed on 7 April.

Membership

  • Total members: 194 (including India).
  • All UN members except Liechtenstein.
  • Also includes Cook Islands and Niue.

Governance Structure

1. World Health Assembly (WHA)

  • Supreme decision-making body.
  • Summit held annually in Geneva.
  • Determines policies of WHO.
  • Appoints the Director-General.

2. Director-General

  • Chief technical and administrative officer of WHO.
  • Elected by WHA through secret ballot.
  • Term: 5 years (renewable once).
  • Current Director-General: Dr. Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus.

3. Executive Board

  • Composed of 34 technically qualified members.
  • Members elected for a term of 3 years.
  • Implements decisions of the World Health Assembly.

Funding Sources

  • Assessed contributions from member states (based on GDP).
  • Voluntary contributions from member states and partners.

Regional Offices

  • WHO Africa Region
  • WHO Western Pacific Region
  • WHO Eastern Mediterranean Region
  • WHO Europe Region
  • WHO Americas Region
  • WHO South-East Asia Region
    • Headquarters: New Delhi, India
    • 11 Member States: Bangladesh, Bhutan, DPR Korea, India, Indonesia, Maldives, Myanmar, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Thailand, Timor-Leste

Objectives

  1. Control and prevent diseases like malaria, TB, HIV, COVID-19.
  2. Improve global health standards.
  3. Issue guidelines for vaccines, medicines, sanitation and food safety.
  4. Support member countries in strengthening health systems.

Key Functions

  1. Setting global health standards.
  2. Coordinating response to global health emergencies.
  3. Supporting vaccination programmes.
  4. Promoting public health campaigns.
  5. Working with UNICEF, World Bank and other UN agencies.

Achievements of WHO

  • Eradication of smallpox.
  • Strengthening global health systems.
  • Tobacco control initiatives.
  • Expansion of immunisation programmes.

Important WHO Reports & Programmes

  • World Health Report
  • World Malaria Report
  • Global TB Report
  • International Health Regulations (IHR)
  • Essential Medicines List

India & WHO

  • India is a founding member of WHO.
  • WHO South-East Asia Regional Office is located in New Delhi.
  • Cooperation in TB control, maternal & child health, and malaria elimination.
  • Collaboration in non-communicable diseases and digital health.
  • WHO–India partnership in traditional medicine.
