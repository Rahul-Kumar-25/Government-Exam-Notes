BIMSTEC

Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation

Basic Information

  • Regional cooperation organisation
  • Established: 1997 (6 June)
  • Initially named BIST-EC
  • Initial members: Bangladesh, India, Sri Lanka, Thailand
  • Headquarters: Dhaka, Bangladesh

Evolution

  • 1997: Formed as BIST-EC by four countries
  • 1997 (22 Dec): Myanmar joined → name changed to BIMSTEC
  • 2004: Nepal and Bhutan joined
  • Full form finalised as Bay of Bengal Initiative for Multi-Sectoral Technical and Economic Cooperation

Members (7)

  • Bangladesh
  • India
  • Myanmar
  • Sri Lanka
  • Thailand
  • Nepal
  • Bhutan

About BIMSTEC

  • Established through the Bangkok Declaration
  • Members lie in the adjacent region of the Bay of Bengal

Aim

  • Create enabling environment for rapid economic development
  • Accelerate social progress
  • Promote collaboration on matters of common regional interest

BIMSTEC Summits

  • 1st Summit: 2004 – Thailand
  • 2nd Summit: 2008 – India
  • 3rd Summit: 2014 – Myanmar
  • 4th Summit: 2018 – Nepal
  • 5th Summit: 2022 – Sri Lanka
  • 6th Summit: 2025 – Thailand

Note: BIMSTEC Charter was finalised at the 5th Summit (2022), Sri Lanka.

Seven Pillars / Priority Areas

Each pillar is led by one member country:

  • Trade & Investment – Bangladesh
  • Environment & Climate Change – Bhutan
  • People-to-People Contact – Nepal
  • Agriculture & Food Security – India, Myanmar
  • Science, Technology & Innovation – Sri Lanka
  • Connectivity – Thailand
  • Security – India
    • Counter-terrorism
    • Disaster management
    • Energy security
    • Transnational crime

Significance of BIMSTEC for India

  • Strategic importance in the Indo-Pacific region
  • Bridges South Asia and South-East Asia
  • Strengthens:
    • Act East Policy
    • Neighbourhood First Policy

Economic Integration

  • Facilitates regional trade and investment
  • Prospect of BIMSTEC Free Trade Agreement

Security Cooperation

  • Counter-terrorism cooperation
  • Maritime security collaboration

Disaster Response

  • Regional cooperation in disaster management

Energy & Climate Cooperation

  • Regional energy projects
  • Renewable energy collaboration

North-East Connectivity

  • India developed Sittwe Port in Myanmar
  • Improves connectivity across the Bay of Bengal

Geopolitical Importance

  • Strengthens India’s presence in the Indo-Pacific region

BIMSTEC vs SAARC

SAARC

  • Limited trade integration
  • India–Pakistan tensions hinder cooperation
  • Slow connectivity progress
  • Minimal security cooperation

BIMSTEC

  • Connects South Asia and South-East Asia
  • Pakistan excluded → smoother political decision-making
  • Connectivity projects:
    • BBIN
    • IMT Highway (India–Myanmar–Thailand)
  • Strong focus on security and maritime cooperation

Challenges of BIMSTEC

  • Slow progress on agreements like BIMSTEC FTA
  • Political instability in some member states (Myanmar, Nepal, Sri Lanka)
  • Overlapping priorities with ASEAN or bilateral engagements
  • Inconsistent summits despite two-year plan
