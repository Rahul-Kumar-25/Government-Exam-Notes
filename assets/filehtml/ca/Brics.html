BRICS

Current Affairs • International Grouping

Headquarters

  • Shanghai, China
  • Annual Summit is held
  • Latest: 17th BRICS Summit hosted by Brazil (Rio de Janeiro)

Aim

  • Bring together major emerging economies
  • Debate on pressing global issues
  • Enhance South–South Cooperation
  • Members are developing countries

Origin

  • 2001: Concept coined by Jim O’Neill
  • Initially called BRIC:
    • Brazil
    • Russia
    • India
    • China
  • 2006: Grouping at Foreign Ministers level
  • 2009: First Summit held in Russia
  • 2010: South Africa joined → BRIC became BRICS

New Development Bank (NDB)

  • Established in 2014
  • Headquarters: Shanghai, China
  • Purpose: Financing infrastructure and sustainable development projects in emerging economies

Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA)

  • Established in 2015
  • Purpose: Provide emergency liquidity to BRICS countries during economic instability

Expansion – BRICS+ (2024)

Original Members (5)

  • Brazil
  • Russia
  • India
  • China
  • South Africa

New Members

  • Egypt
  • Ethiopia
  • Iran
  • UAE
  • Indonesia
  • Saudi Arabia (membership on hold)

Note: Argentina initially accepted membership but later declined.

Reasons for Expansion

1. Strategic / Global Influence

  • Create counterbalance to Western dominance
  • Potential new global power centre
  • Counterbalance to G7
  • Strengthening voice of Global South

2. Economic Strength

  • Strengthens economic potential of members
  • Promotes trade and investment opportunities

3. Energy Security

  • Inclusion of Iran, UAE and Saudi Arabia
  • Access to major energy reserves

4. Geopolitical Significance

  • Shipping → cheapest
  • Railways → more expensive than shipping
  • Road → most expensive
  • Egypt and Ethiopia improve access to maritime trade routes

Significance of BRICS+

  • Voice of Global South – counterbalance to G7
  • Rising global influence and resilience
  • Represents:
    • ~49.5% of world population (latest ~56%)
    • ~40% of global GDP (latest ~44%)
  • Includes major oil producers like UAE and Iran

17th BRICS Summit

Two Central Pillars

  • Global South Cooperation
  • BRICS partnership for social, economic and environmental development

Outcomes

  • Global Health: Equitable healthcare access
  • Trade & Finance: Encouraging de-dollarization
  • Trade: Promoting use of local currency
  • Climate Change: BRICS climate leadership & green financing
  • AI Governance: Ethical, inclusive and innovative AI development
  • Peace & Security: UN reforms, counter-terrorism, conflict resolution
  • Institutional Development: Strengthening internal mechanisms

Challenges

  • Difficulty in countering dollar dominance
  • Geopolitical contradictions among new members
  • Complex decision-making due to expansion
