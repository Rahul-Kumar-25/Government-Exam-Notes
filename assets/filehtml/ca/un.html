United Nations (UN)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

UN Organisation

  • Intergovernmental organisation
  • Headquarters: New York, USA
  • Established to promote international cooperation and maintain peace

UN Charter

  • Preamble + 111 Articles
  • Divided into 19 Chapters

Objectives of UN

  1. Develop friendly relations among nations
  2. Promote respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms
  3. International cooperation in economic, social, cultural and humanitarian fields
  4. Act as a centre for harmonising actions of nations

Evolution of UN

  • 1920: League of Nations formed
  • 1941: Atlantic Charter
  • 26 June 1945: UN Charter signed by 51 countries
  • 24 October 1945: UN officially came into existence
  • 1946: First meeting held in London
  • 1948: Universal Declaration of Human Rights adopted

Members

  • Founding Members: 51
  • Current Members: 193
  • Last member added (2011): South Sudan

Observer States

  • Vatican City
  • Palestine

Official Languages

  • Arabic
  • Chinese
  • English
  • French
  • Russian
  • Spanish

Six Principal Organs of UN

  1. General Assembly
  2. Security Council
  3. Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)
  4. Secretariat
  5. Trusteeship Council
  6. International Court of Justice (ICJ)

General Assembly (UNGA)

  • Headquarters: New York, USA
  • All 193 members participate
  • Main deliberative and policy-making body
  • Meets once every year

Security Council (UNSC)

  • Headquarters: New York, USA
  • Responsible for international peace and security
  • Total Members: 15
  • Permanent Members (P5): USA, UK, France, Russia, China
  • Non-Permanent Members: 10 (2-year term)
  • Veto power only with permanent members
  • India elected 8 times (last: 2021–22)

ECOSOC

  • Headquarters: New York, USA
  • Coordinates economic, social and environmental issues
  • 54 members elected for 3-year term

International Court of Justice (ICJ)

  • Headquarters: The Hague, Netherlands
  • Judicial organ of UN
  • 15 judges with 9-year term
  • Settles disputes between states

Secretariat

  • Headquarters: New York, USA
  • Handles daily administration of UN
  • Headed by UN Secretary-General
  • Current Secretary-General: António Guterres

Trusteeship Council

  • Formed to administer trust territories
  • Suspended operations in 1994
  • Last trust territory: Palau

Specialised Agencies of UN

  • WHO
  • UNESCO
  • UNICEF
  • IMF
  • World Bank
  • ILO
  • UNDP
  • FAO

India and UN

  • India is a founding member
  • Major contributor to UN peacekeeping (Blue Helmets)
  • Supports UN reforms and demand permanent seat in UNSC
  • Hosted first internation yoga day with UN support
  • Dr. B. N. Rau was the indian representative of UN Charter drafting committee

Significance of UN

  • Promotion of peace and security
  • Humanitarian assistance
  • Support for development
  • Promotion of democracy

Challenges of UN

  • Inability to prevent some conflicts
  • Limited Effectiveness
  • Funding constraints
  • Limited authority over sovereign states
  • Decision-making delays

Important UN-Related Days

  • 24 October – UN Day
  • 20 June – World Refugee Day
  • 7 April – World Health Day
  • 10 December – Human Rights Day
  • 5 June – World Environment Day
  • 8 March – International Women’s Day
