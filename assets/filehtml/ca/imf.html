International Monetary Fund (IMF)

Current Affairs • International Organisation

Headquarters

Washington DC, USA

Introduction

  • Global financial institution.
  • Established in 1944.
  • Promotes international economic cooperation and financial stability.
  • Founded at the Bretton Woods Conference.
  • Came into existence in 1945.
  • Financial operations started in 1947.

Membership

  • Founding members: 29 (including India)
  • Current members: 191

Objectives of IMF

  1. Address balance of payment (BoP) issues.
  2. Promote exchange rate stability.
  3. Facilitate balanced growth of international trade.
  4. Establish a multilateral payment system.
  5. Provide loans during economic crises in exchange for reforms.

Functions of IMF

  • Surveillance: Monitors global economic trends and provides policy advice.
  • Financial Assistance: Short-term loans for BoP crisis.
  • Capacity Development: Technical assistance and training.

IMF Quota & Voting Power

  • Each member contributes a quota based on economic size.
  • Quota determines voting power and access to IMF loans.
  • India’s quota: ~2.75%
  • Largest shareholders: USA, Japan, China, Germany, UK.

SDR – Special Drawing Rights

  • International reserve asset created in 1969.
  • Supplement to official reserves of member countries.
  • Based on a basket of five currencies:
    • US Dollar
    • Euro
    • Chinese Yuan
    • Japanese Yen
    • British Pound
  • India’s SDR holding: ~13 billion SDR
  • Latest SDR allocation: 2021 (COVID-19 recovery)

IMF Lending Programmes

  1. Stand-By Arrangement (SBA) – Short-term BoP support.
  2. Extended Fund Facility (EFF) – Medium to long-term support.
  3. PRGT – Concessional loans for low-income countries.
  4. Rapid Financing Instrument (RFI) – Emergency assistance.
  5. Rapid Credit Facility (RCF) – Emergency loans for low-income countries.
  6. Flexible Credit Line (FCL) – For countries with strong fundamentals.
  7. Precautionary & Liquidity Line (PLL) – For vulnerable economies.
  8. Resilience & Sustainability Facility (RSF) – Introduced in 2022.

India and IMF

  • India is a founding member.
  • Received IMF assistance during the 1991 BoP crisis.
  • Active role in global financial policy discussions.
  • Current Managing Director: Kristalina Georgieva.
