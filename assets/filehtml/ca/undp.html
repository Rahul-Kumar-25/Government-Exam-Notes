UNDP (United Nations Development Programme)

UN Development Programme

Headquarters: New York (USA)

  • UNDP is the world’s largest development agency within the United Nations system.
  • UNDP plays a leading role in helping nations work towards the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), focusing on fairness, human dignity and long-term growth.
  • Established: 1965
  • Operations began: 1966
  • Members: 177 (including India)
  • UNDP is not a specialized agency; it is a programme of the UN General Assembly.

Evolution

  • UNDP emerged from the merger of EPTA (Expanded Programme of Technical Assistance) and UN Special Fund (SF).
  • EPTA was formed in 1949 and focused on economic assistance and capacity building for developing countries.
  • The Special Fund was created in 1958 to prepare nations for long-term development by supporting large-scale technical projects.
  • The merger was recommended by the UN ECOSOC to reduce duplication of tasks and make development support more systematic.
  • By 1966, the merger was completed and UNDP began functioning as the primary global development agency of the UN.

Objectives

  • Eradicate poverty
  • Reduce inequality
  • Promote sustainable development
  • Strengthen democratic governance
  • Support climate action and resilience
  • Promote peace and crisis recovery
  • Focus on people-centric development

Core Areas of Work

Poverty Reduction and Human Development

  • Inclusive economic growth
  • Employment generation
  • Social protection
  • Access to basic services

Democratic Governance

  • Strengthening institutions
  • Free and fair elections
  • Rule of law
  • Transparency and accountability
  • Anti-corruption reforms

Funding of UNDP

  • UNDP is funded by voluntary contributions.
  • Contributions come from:
    • UN member states
    • International organizations
    • Private sector

UNDP and India

UNDP works closely with the Government of India (GOI).

Focus Areas in India
