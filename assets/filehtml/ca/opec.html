OPEC

Organisation of Petroleum Exporting Countries • Current Affairs

Formation

  • Established in 1960
  • Headquarters: Vienna, Austria
  • First five years (1960–1965): Geneva, Switzerland
  • Permanent intergovernmental organisation
  • Formed at the Baghdad Conference
  • Founding members:
    • Iran
    • Iraq
    • Kuwait
    • Saudi Arabia
    • Venezuela

Aim

  • Manage supply of oil to influence world oil prices
  • Avoid price fluctuations affecting:
    • Oil-producing countries
    • Oil-consuming countries

OPEC Membership

  • Open to countries that are substantial exporters of oil
  • Members must share the ideals of the organisation

Objectives

  • Coordinate and unify petroleum policies among member countries
  • Secure fair and stable prices for petroleum producers
  • Ensure efficient and regular supply of petroleum to consumers
  • Ensure fair return on capital for investors in petroleum industry

Functions

  • Bring stability to the global petroleum market
  • Review status of the international oil market
  • Forecast future developments in oil demand and prices
  • Match oil production with expected global demand
  • Accounts for 81.5% of world’s proven oil reserves

OPEC+

  • Includes non-OPEC oil-exporting countries coordinating with OPEC
  • Major OPEC+ members:
    • Russia
    • Azerbaijan
    • Bahrain
    • Brunei
    • Kazakhstan
    • Malaysia
    • Mexico
    • Oman
    • South Sudan
    • Sudan

Asian Premium

  • Asian countries are charged $1–$2 higher prices for crude oil
  • Compared to Europe and the US
  • This price difference is known as Asian Premium
