ASEAN

Association of South East Asian Nations • Current Affairs

Basic Information

  • Idea: 1961
  • Established: 1967
  • Place: Bangkok, Thailand
  • Declaration: Bangkok Declaration (ASEAN Declaration)
  • Objective: Promote economic, cultural and social cooperation

Founding Members (1967)

  • Indonesia
  • Philippines
  • Singapore
  • Malaysia
  • Thailand

Later Members

  • Brunei
  • Vietnam
  • Laos
  • Myanmar
  • Cambodia

Total Members: 10
New Member: East Timor (now total members: 11)

Important Summits & Developments

  • 1st Summit (1976): Bali, Indonesia
    • Treaty of Amity and Cooperation
    • Aim: Peaceful relations
  • 2015: ASEAN Economic Community (AEC) formed
    • Aim: Economic integration and regional growth
  • 2020: Joined RCEP
    • World’s largest Free Trade Agreement

Motto / Vision

  • “One Vision, One Identity, One Community”
  • Building economic, political and socio-cultural community

Headquarters

  • Secretariat: Jakarta, Indonesia

Functioning

  • Summit: Held annually, sets policy direction
  • Chairmanship: Rotational (Alphabetical order)
  • Decision Making: Consultation and consensus

Ministerial Councils

  • Political-Security Council
  • Economic Council
  • Socio-Cultural Council
  • Community Council

Fundamental Principles

  • Mutual respect
  • Respect for sovereignty and territorial integrity
  • Non-interference in internal affairs
  • Peaceful settlement of disputes
  • Effective cooperation

Strengths

  • Regional stability and peace
  • Large population base (≈662+ million)
  • World’s 4th largest exporter
  • Cultural diversity
  • Strategic location (Strait of Malacca)
  • Strong diplomatic arrangements

Opportunities for India

  • Market access: 3rd largest in Asia, 5th in the world
  • Strategic convergence:
    • Indo-Pacific Strategy
    • Act East Policy
  • Countering China’s influence
  • Improved connectivity with North-East Asia

India–ASEAN Cooperation Areas

  • Trade and Investment (FTA signed)
  • Defence and Security
  • Socio-cultural exchange
  • Education and Research
  • Funding ASEAN–India projects

Challenges: India & ASEAN

  • Trade deficit
  • Chinese presence
  • Limited connectivity
    • Physical and digital connectivity gaps
    • Affects trade, investment and people-to-people ties
  • Nature of engagement mainly bilateral
