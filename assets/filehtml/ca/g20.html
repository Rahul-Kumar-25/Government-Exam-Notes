G20 (Group of Twenty)

Current Affairs • International Forum

Introduction

  • G20 is an intergovernmental forum.
  • Established in 1999.
  • Formed after the Asian Financial Crisis (1997–98).
  • Aims to strengthen global financial stability.
  • Became a leaders’ summit forum in 2008 after the Global Financial Crisis.
  • In 2009, designated as the “Premier Forum for International Economic Cooperation.”
  • Initially focused on macroeconomic issues, later expanded to health, energy, environment, climate change, sustainable development and anti-corruption.

Members

Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, UK, USA, European Union.

  • In 2023, the African Union was added as a permanent member.
  • Total members: 21 (in practice).

Objectives of G20

  1. Promote international financial stability and growth.
  2. Strengthen global economic cooperation.
  3. Address climate change and sustainable development.
  4. Improve trade, innovation and job creation.
  5. Ensure an inclusive and resilient global economy.
  6. Discuss health, energy, food security and digital transformation.

Working of G20

  • No permanent headquarters or secretariat.
  • Presidency rotates annually among member countries.
  • Presiding country sets the agenda and hosts the summit.
  • Works through two parallel tracks:

1. Finance Track

  • Led by Finance Ministers and Central Bank Governors.

2. Sherpa Track

  • Representatives appointed by Heads of Government.

Other Features

  • Engagement groups include civil society, think tanks, women, youth, labour, business and researchers.
  • Troika system: Previous, current and incoming presidencies.
  • G20 is a non-binding forum.
  • Works closely with IMF, World Bank and WTO.

G20 Summits

  • Held annually.
  • First summit: 2008, Washington DC.
  • USA hosted twice (2008 & 2009).
  • 2023: 18th Summit – India
  • 2024: Brazil
  • 2025: Johannesburg, South Africa

Importance of G20

  • Represents about 85% of global GDP.
  • Accounts for 75% of global trade.
  • Represents nearly 66% of world population.

India and G20

  • India hosted G20 in 2023.
  • Theme: “Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam” (One Earth, One Family, One Future).
  • Logo inspired by Lotus and Earth with Indian tricolour.
  • Summit venue: Bharat Mandapam, New Delhi.

Outcomes of India’s G20 Presidency

  1. African Union became a permanent member.
  2. Launch of India–Middle East–Europe Economic Corridor (IMEC).
  3. Focus on green development, climate finance and digital public infrastructure.
  4. Emphasis on women-led development.
  5. Promotion of sustainable and inclusive growth.
  6. Positioned India as the voice of the Global South.
