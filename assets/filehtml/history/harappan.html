Harappan or Indus Valley Civilisation

Time Period: 2500 BC – 1750 BC

  • The oldest name is Indus Civilisation
  • The most appropriate name is Harappan Civilisation
  • John Marshall was the first scholar to use the term Indus Civilisation
  • Belongs to the Proto-historic period
  • Spread over Sindh, Baluchistan, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Western UP and Northern Maharashtra
  • Harappa – Ghaggar – Mohenjodaro represents the heartland of Indus civilisation

Extreme Sites

  • Northernmost site: Earlier – Ropar (near Sutlej), Now – Manda (near Chenab)
  • Southernmost site: Earlier – Bhagatrav, Now – Daimabad
  • Easternmost site: Alamgirpur
  • Westernmost site: Sutkagendor

Capital Cities

Harappa and Mohenjodaro

Port Cities

Lothal, Sutkagendor, Allahdino, Balakot and Kuntasi (Gujarat)

Common Features of Major Cities

  • Grid pattern town planning
  • Use of burnt bricks
  • Well-developed underground drainage system
  • Fortified citadel (Exception: Chanhudaro)

Agriculture & Animals

  • Main crops: Wheat, Barley
  • Rice cultivation evidence only from Lothal and Rangpur
  • First producers of cotton in the world
  • Animals: Sheep, Goat, Oxen, Buffalo, Dog, Cat, Pig, Deer, Elephant, Camel, Rhinoceros, Tiger
  • Lion was not known to Indus people

Trade

Both inland and foreign trade existed. Major trade relations were with Mesopotamia (Sumeria – Iraq) and Bahrain.

Imports

  • Gold – Kolar, Afghanistan, Persia
  • Silver – Afghanistan, Persia, South India
  • Copper – Baluchistan, Arabia
  • Tin – Afghanistan, Bihar
  • Lapis Lazuli – Afghanistan
  • Jade – Central Asia
  • Steatite – Iran, Kirthar Hills

Exports

  • Agricultural products
  • Cotton goods
  • Terracotta figurines
  • Pottery
  • Ivory and conch shell products

Currency

No coins were found. Barter system was used.

Polity

No clear evidence. Ruling authority appears to be a class of merchants.

Religion

  • No temple worship
  • Mother Goddess (Shakti) worship
  • Pasupati Mahadeva (Proto-Shiva) worship
  • Yoni and Lingam worship
  • Animal and tree worship

Script

  • Pictographic script found mainly on seals
  • Script not deciphered
  • Boustrophedon style of writing
  • Referred to as Proto-Dravidian

Important Notes

  • Steatite used for seals
  • Humpless bull common on seals
  • Swastika symbol traced to Indus civilisation
  • Contemporary civilisations: Mesopotamia, Egypt, China
