Mauryan empire

Maurya Mauryan Empire

322 BC to 185 BC

Sources of Mauryan history:

(a) Literary Sources:

  • (i) Arthashastra by (Kautilya – चाणक्य (Chanakya))
    • deals with administration, economy, espionage (गुप्तचर),
    • law, taxation
  • (ii) Indica (by Megasthenes) Greek ambassador
    • describe society, administration, caste system
  • (iii) Puranas
  • (iv) Buddhist text – Dipavamsa, Mahavamsa
  • (v) Jain text

(b) Archaeological Sources:

  • (i) Ashokan edicts (major and minor rock edicts)
  • (ii) Pillars of Ashoka (Sarnath, Lauria Nandangarh)
  • (iii) Stupas (Sanchi stupa, Bharhut)

Formation of Mauryan Empire:

  • (a) founded by Chandragupta Maurya in 322 BC
  • (b) overthrew the Nanda Dynasty
  • (c) assisted by Chanakya
  • (d) extent:
    • (i) first empire to unify almost entire Indian subcontinent
    • (ii) capital was Pataliputra

Rulers of Mauryan Empire:

1. Chandragupta Maurya (322 BC – 298 BC)

  • defeated Seleucus Nicator (Greek ruler)
  • Treaty:
    • (305 BC)
    • (a) Chandragupta received Afghanistan and Baluchistan
    • (b) Seleucus gave his daughter in marriage
  • Megasthenes sent as ambassador
  • Converted to Jainism
  • died at Shravanabelagola

2. Bindusara (298 BC to 273 BC)

  • son of Chandragupta Maurya
  • known as Amitraghata
  • maintained friendly relation with Greeks

3. Ashoka the Great (273 BC to 232 BC)

  • first ruler to maintain direct contact with his people through inscriptions
  • organised the third Buddhist council at Pataliputra
  • appointed Dhamma Mahamatras to propagate Dhamma among various social groups including women
  • was a great proponent of Buddhism
  • his empire covered the whole territory from Hindu Kush to Bengal
  • he banned animal sacrifices, regulated the slaughter of animals for food
  • established Dharmashalas, hospitals throughout his kingdom

Kalinga War (261 BC):

  • (i) turning point of Ashoka life
  • (ii) massive bloodshed
  • (iii) after war embraced Buddhism
  • (iv) adopted policy of Dhamma

4. Brihadratha

last ruler of Mauryan empire

Ashoka’s Dhamma:

  • (i) Principles:
    • non violence
    • respect for elders
    • religious tolerance
    • kindness to slaves & servants
  • (ii) moral conduct
  • (iii) propagation:
    • through edicts
    • appointed Dhamma Mahamatras

Note: Dhamma was not Buddhism, it was ethical code, not religious doctrine

Ashokan Edicts:

  • (i) Types:
    • major rock edicts (14)
    • minor rock edicts
    • pillar edicts
  • (ii) Languages used:
    • Prakrit – official language of Mauryan empire
    • Greek and Aramaic
  • (iii) Important Edicts:
    • 13th rock edict → Kalinga war के बाद
    • Pillar edict VII → Dhamma policy

Note: National emblem of India is from Sarnath lion capital

Mauryan Administration:

  • (i) Central Administration:
    • King was supreme authority
    • Council of ministers (मंत्री परिषद)
  • (ii) Provincial Administration:
    • Empire divided into provinces
    • headed by Kumara (Royal Prince)
  • (iii) District and villages:
    • Sthanikas (District officer)
    • Gopa (Village officer)
  • (iv) Espionage system:
    • Highly developed
    • mentioned in Arthashastra

Army:

  • Standing army
  • Six departments:
    • (i) Infantry
    • (ii) Cavalry
    • (iii) Elephants
    • (iv) Chariots
    • (v) Navy
    • (vi) Transport

Economy:

  • (i) Agriculture:
    • main occupation
    • state owned land
    • irrigation tax
  • (ii) Taxes:
    • Bhaga – land tax (1/6th of produce)
    • Toll tax, trade tax
  • (iii) Trade:
    • internal and external trade flourished
    • use of punch marked coins

Art and Culture:

  • (i) Architecture:
    • wooden structures
    • pillar with polished surface
  • (ii) Sculpture:
    • Lion capital of Sarnath
    • Yaksha and Yakshini figures

Religion and Society:

  • Buddhism spread widely
  • third Buddhist council held
  • Ashoka sent missionaries to Sri Lanka, Central Asia, South-East Asia

Decline of Mauryan Empire:

  • weak successors after Ashoka
  • large empire difficult to administer
  • financial strain
  • Brahmanical reaction against Buddhism
  • last ruler was Brihadratha
  • he was killed by Pushyamitra Shunga
