Post Mauryan or Pre Gupta Period

1. Shunga Dynasty (185 BC to 73 BC)

(a) Founder → Pushyamitra Shunga

  • killed last Mauryan ruler Brihadratha
  • capital was Pataliputra

Important features:

  • (i) Revival of Brahmanism
  • (ii) Performed Ashvamedha Yagya
  • (iii) Decline in Buddhist royal patronage

Cultural Contributions:

  • (i) Bharhut Stupa enlargement
  • (ii) Development of Sanskrit literature

Note: Cruelty towards Buddhists

(b) Agnimitra – 2nd ruler

(c) Bhagabhadra – 3rd ruler

(d) Devabhuti – 4th ruler – last ruler of Shunga Dynasty

– killed by his Brahmin minister Vasudeva Kanva

2. Kanva Dynasty (73 BC to 28 BC)

  • founded by Vasudeva Kanva
  • last Shunga ruler killed by him
  • short lived dynasty
  • ended by Satavahanas

3. Indo Greeks (Yavanas) (2nd century BC)

  • capital – Taxila
  • most famous ruler – Menander (Milinda)

Note: They were first to issue gold coins

Contributions:

  • (i) Patronised Buddhism
  • (ii) Text – Milinda Panha
  • (iii) Introduced Greek styled coins

4. Shakas (1st century BC to 4th century AD) (Scythians)

(i) Administrative System:

  • introduced Kshatrapa system
  • Ex. Governor – Maha Kshatrapa
  • (ii) Famous ruler – Rudradaman I
  • (iii) Important inscription – Junagarh inscription – written in pure Sanskrit
  • (iv) Other important Shaka rulers were:
    • Nahapana
    • Ushavadeva
    • Chashtana etc.

5. Parthians (Pahlavas) (1st century BC – 1st century AD)

  • ruled small territory in North-West India
  • important ruler – Gondophernes
  • promoted trade and culture

6. Kushan Dynasty (1st century AD – 3rd century AD)

  • (i) Founder → Kujula Kadphises
  • (ii) Greatest ruler → Kanishka
  • (iii) Capital → Purushapura (Peshawar)

Kanishka Contribution:

  • Patron of Mahayana Buddhism
  • 4th Buddhist Council

Note: Kushans were the first ruler in India to issue gold coins on a wide scale.

They controlled the famous Silk Route (China to Europe)

Satavahanas (1st century BC to 3rd century AD)

Origin & Territory:

  • (i) Founded in Deccan region
  • (ii) Core areas are MH, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana
  • (iii) Capital city – Pratishthana and Amaravati

Important Rulers:

  • (i) Simuka – founder of Satavahanas – patron of Jainism & Brahmanism
  • (ii) Satakarni I
  • (iii) Gautamiputra Satakarni – greatest ruler
    • defeated the Shaka ruler Nahapana
    • extended the empire from Malwa to Karnataka
    • known from Nasik inscription by his mother Gautami Balashri
  • (iv) Vashishthiputra Pulumayi
    • maintained stability & trade
    • shifted capital to Paithan
    • according to Junagarh inscription he married the daughter of Rudradaman I
  • (v) Yajnasri Satakarni – recovered area North Konkan and Malwa from Shakas ruler

Administration:

  • monarchy system
  • king assisted by ministers
  • provinces called Ahara
  • officials known as Amatyas
  • military included infantry, cavalry, elephants etc.

Economy:

  • agriculture was the backbone
  • promoted irrigation (tanks and canals)
  • active trade with Roman Empire (Roman gold coins found in Deccan)

Coinage:

  • issued copper coins, silver coins and lead coins
  • first Indian ruler to:
    • use bilingual inscription
    • use portrait coins
  • symbols – ship, elephant etc.

Religion:

  • (i) followed Brahmanism
  • (ii) also patronised Buddhism & Jainism
  • (iii) built stupas, viharas, chaityas etc.

Social System:

  • (i) varna system existed but less rigid
  • (ii) women enjoyed respectable status
  • (iii) matronymic naming (ex: Gotami Putra)

Decline:

  • (i) internal conflicts
  • (ii) decline by 3rd century

Note: Language was Prakrit, Amaravati School of Art

Features:

Gandhara

  • Spiritual Buddha
  • Sad Buddha
  • Bearded Buddha
  • Buddha in yogic posture
  • Depicted with garment draped in Graeco-Roman fashion
  • wavy hair with large forehead and long ears
  • Dhyana mudra
  • Abhaya mudra
  • Bhoomi Sparsha mudra
  • Dharma Chakra mudra
  • Halo not decorated

Mathura

  • Delighted Buddha
  • lacking spiritual look
  • with no beard or moustaches
  • shaved head and face
  • sitted in Padma asana
  • halo around the Buddha was decorated with geometrical patterns
  • surrounded by 2 monks
  • having strong muscular structure

Amaravati

  • Natural look
  • mainly found on the railings and other parts of stupas
