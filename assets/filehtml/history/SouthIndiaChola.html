South India Cholas and others

South India Chola and others

8th century - 12th century

  • capital - Tanjore, Gangaikondacholapuram
  • founder - Vijayalaya
  • Greatest Ruler - Rajaraja and Rajendra - I
  • Arrangement of local self govt. is regarded as a basic feature of Chola
  • Land Revenue and trade tax were main source of income
  • King was the head of central authority helped by council of ministers (democratic)
  • Rajendra - I conquered Odisha, Bengal, Berma and Andaman and Nicobar Island
  • Rajendra - I assumed the title Gangaikondachola and built a city called Gangaikcholapuram.
  • Style of Architecture was Dravida
  • Kambana, Kuttana and Pugalendi are considered as three gems of Tamil poetry
  • image making which reached its climate in the dancing figure of Shiva called Nataraja
  • Kambana who wrote Ramavataram, Kamba Ramayan
  • Rajaraja build Brihadeshwar / Rajarajeshwar temple at Tanjore at tribute to Shiva
  • In Dravid temples the Vimana or Tall pyramidal tower dominate the whole structure of the Shrina. Gukoram and Garabhagriha are imp. structure of that time.
