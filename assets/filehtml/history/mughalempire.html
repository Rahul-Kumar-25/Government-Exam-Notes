Mughal Empire

MUGHAL EMPIRE (1526 - 1857)

Sources of Mughal History:

  • Babar Nama (by Babar)
  • Humayunnama (by Gulbadan Begum)
  • Akbar Nama & Ain-i-Akbari (by Abdul Fazal)
  • Tuzuk-i-Jahangiri (by Zahangir)
  • Alamgir nama (by Aurangzeb)
  • Maasir-i-Alamgiri (by Saqi mustang khan)

1. Babur (1526 - 1530)

  • Founder of mughal Empire
  • Decended of Timore & Chenghiz Khan
  • Introduced Gun powder & Artillary warfare in india
  • Real name - Zahar-ud-din-mohammad Babur

important Battles

  1. First Battle of Panipat (1526)
    • Defeated Ibrahim lodhi
  2. Battle of Khanwa (1527)
    • Defeated Ranasang
  3. Battle of chanderi (1528)
    • Defeated medini Rao
  4. Battle of Ghagra (1529)
    • Defeated Afghans

2. Humayu (1530 - 1540) & (1555 - 1556)

  • Son of Babur
  • lost empire to Shenshah Suri
  • Defeated in :
    • a. Battle of chousa (1539)
    • b. Battle of Kannauj / Bilgram (1540)
  • took Refuge in persia (Iran)
  • Recaptured throne in 1555
  • Died due to fall from stairs of library (Dinpanah)

Shershah Suri (1540 - 1545)

  • Founder of Sur Empire
  • introduced Rupiya at that time it was silver coin
  • Strong land Revenue system introduced.
  • Built Grand trunk road (Sonargaon to Peshawar)
  • Efficient postal system.
  • Divided empire into Sarkar & Parganas
  • Built Purana Killa at Delhi
  • was succeeded by Islam Shah (1545 - 1554) and Islam Shah by mohd. Adil shah

3. Akbar (1556 - 1605)

  • Akbar was Great Ruler
  • Elder son of Humayu
  • Title : "Jalaluddin mohammad Akbar Badshah Gazi"
  • Second Battle of Panipat (1556)
    • defeated Hemu & Behram khan
  • mansabdari system (Administration)
  • Abolished Jizya tax & pilgrimage tax
  • His religious policy Sulh-i-kul (it means universal tolerance)
  • founded Din-i-ilahi (1582) (not a religious only a Ethical court)
  • Built Fatehpur sikri, Agra fort, Lahore fort, Allahabad fort, Humayu tomb
  • Birbal was the only Hindu who followed his "Dil-i-ilahi"

Navratnas (9 gems of Akbar)

  1. Birbal (Administrator)
  2. Abul Fazl (Scholar & states man)
  3. Faizi (Scholar & states man)
  4. Todar mal (Finance minister)
  5. Bhagwandas (He was mansabdar)
  6. man Singh (mansabdar)
  7. Tansen (musician)
  8. Abdul Rahim khan-i-khana (states man & Hindi poet)
  9. Mulla Do-piyaza (advisor)
  • Birbal was killed in the Battle with the Yasufzai tribe in 1586.
  • Abdul Fazl was murdered by Bundela (1601)
  • Akbar gave Mughal India one official language i.e. Persian
  • He is considered the real founder of mughal Empire in India
  • when Akbar died he was Burried at Sikandra near Agra

4. Jahagir (1605 - 1627)

  • Son of Akbar
  • Real name : "Salim"
  • wrote Tuzuk-i-Jehagiri
  • introduced "chain of Justice"
  • Title - Nur-ud-din Muhammad i.e. conquerer of world
  • married mehrunisa (Sher Afghani's widow)
  • Her title of Noor Jahan (most powerful queen)
  • Title of "Itmaduddaula" given by mehrunisa's father
  • Art & painting flourished
  • Noorjha was made official padshah Begum
  • He wrote his memories "tuzuk-i-zahangiri in Persian"
  • He was burried in Lahore.
  • Noor jahan was officially called "Badshah Begum" (Captain Hawkins)

5. Shah Jahan (1628 - 1658)

  • Golden age of mughal Architecture
  • major construction
    • Taj mahal (Agra)
    • Red fort (Delhi)
    • Jama masjid
    • Moti masjid
  • Son of Jahagir
  • Removed by Aurangzeb

6. Aurangzeb (1658 - 1707)

  • Last powerful Mughal Emperor
  • Real name : "Alamgir"
  • Expanded empire to maximum extent.
  • Re-imposed Jizya
  • He was called "Zinda Peer" (the living Saint)
  • Orthodox Sunni Muslim
  • Deccan wars weaken Empire.

Administrative Issues:

  • continuous wars
  • Revolt of marathas, sikhs, Jats, Rajputs etc.

Later Mughals (1707 - 1857):

  • Bahadur Shah - I (1707 - 1712)
  • Farrukhsiyar (1713 - 1719)
  • muhammad Shah (1719 - 1748) "Rangeela"
    • Nadir Shah invaded India in 1739
    • Looted Peacock throne & Koh-i-Noor (Takate-Taj)
  • Ahmad Shah Abdali (1748 - 1754)
  • Alamgir - II (1754 - 1759)
  • Shah Alam II (1759 - 1806)
  • Akbar II (1806 - 1837)
  • Bahadur Shah - II (Bahadur Shah Zafar) (1837 - 1857)

Decline of mughal empire:

  • weak successors who were incompetent both as administrator & general
  • war of succession
  • Aurangzebs, Rajputs, deccan & Religious policies
  • Factionalism among nobility after Aurangzeb
  • Growth of marathas and other regional powers in Bengal, Hyderabad, Awadh, Mysore etc.
  • Foreign invasions by Nadir shah & Abdali
  • British conquest of India

Mughal Administration

mansabdari System

  • introduced by Akbar
  • Rank based system
  • Zat - Personal Rank
  • Sawar - Number of Cavalry to maintain
  • officer is mansabdar

Jagirdari System

  • Assignment of Revenue of a particular territory to the nobles
  • Collected in Cash
  • officer is Jagir

Note: mughal mansabdar formed the highest paid service in the world.

Art and Culture:

  • "Persian" language dominant
  • miniature paintings flourished
  • Music Patronized
