South India Vijayanagar Empire

South India

VIJAY NAGAR EMPIRE (1336 - 1646)

Foundation of the empire :

  1. founded in 1336 AD
  2. founder - Hari Hara I & Bukkaraya I
  3. capital - Vijay Nagar on the bank of tungbhadra
  4. founded with the support of sage Vidyaranya
  5. Purpose - to check the expansion of Delhi Sultanate in south India
  6. Protect Hindu culture, temple & traditions

Dynasties of Vijay Nagar Empire :

1. Sangam Dynasty (1336 - 1485)

founder - Harihara I & Bukkaraya I

important rulers -

  • Harihara I - consolidated the empire
  • Bukkaraya I - expanded territory, pattern of learning
  • Devaraya I - strengthen army recruited muslims
  • Devaraya II - most powerful of Sangam Ruler called Gajabetekara (The elephant hunter)

2. Saluva Dynasty (1485 - 1505)

  • founder : Saluva narasimha
  • come to power due to weakness of sangam ruler
  • very short lived dynasty

3. Tuluva Dynasty (1505 - 1570)

  • founder - Vira Narasimha
  • Greatest Ruler - Krishan Devaraya

Krishan Devaraya (1509 - 1529)

  • Golden Age of vijaynagar empire
  • titles -
    • "Yavanarajya sthapanacharya"
    • "Andhrabhoja"
    • "Abhinav Bhoja"
    • "Alharapithama"
  • Achievements - Defeated Gajapatis of odisha
  • Controlled Raichur Doab
  • friendly Relation with Portugies
  • Patron of literature and Art
  • He Build the Vijay Mahal, Hazara Ram temple & Vittalswami temple.

4. Aravido Dynasty (1570 - 1646)

  • founder - Tirumala devaraya
  • last dynasty of the empire
  • Gradual decline due to weak Rulers

Administration

  • King was supreme Authority
  • Empire divided into Mandalam, nado (dist.), sthala (town), gram (village)
  • important official - Maha pradhan (PM)
  • Danayaka (military officer)
  • Rayasam (Secretary)

Military System

  • Strong army with infantry, cavalry, elephant etc.
  • Horses imported from Arab traders & portugies
  • Use of fire Arms
  • forts were well protected

Economy

  • Agriculture was backbone
  • Advanced irrigation system like tanks, canals, dams etc.
  • major crops were - Rice, Sugar cane, spices etc.
  • flourishing trade & commerce
  • Ports - Goa (Machilipatnam)
  • Gold coins - (Pagoda) or (Varaha)

Society

  • Based on varna system
  • position of women - some women were educated
  • practice of sati existed
  • Devdasi system privlent
  • Emphasis on Dharam & Rituals

Religion

  • Rulers were Hindu
  • Patronised Vaishnism & Shaivism
  • Also tolerance toward Islam
  • Construction & Renovation of temples

Art & Architecture

  • Dravid style
  • Use of tall Gopurams, mandapas & pillars
  • famous monuments - Virupaksha temple, Vittal Swami temple, Hazara rama temple, Lotus mahal, Elephants stable

Literature

  • language patronised - Sanskrit, Telgu, kanar and Tamil
  • "Ashtadiggajas" (eight poets of Telgu literature)
  • led by Allasami veddone
  • Krishna devaraya himself wrote "Amuktamalyada"

Battle of Talikota (1565)

  • Also called Battle of "Rakshasa Tangadi"
  • fought b/w Vijay Nagar Empire & Deccan Sultanate (Bijapur, Golkonda, Ahmadnagar and Vidar)
  • Result - Vijaynagar army defeated & Hampi was destroyed.

Decline of empire

  • Defeat in Battle of Talikota
  • weak successor
  • internal conflicts
  • pressure from Deccan Sultanate

imp. foreign Traveller

  • Nicolo Conti (Italian)
  • Domingo Paes (portuguese)
  • Fernao Nuniz (portugue)
