Chalcolithic Age (3500 BC – 1000 BC)

Copper + Stone

  • Men used copper implements along with stone implements.
  • This age is known as chalco-lithic age, also called neo-lithic chalco-lithic age.
  • First metal to make implements was copper (used first around 5000 BC).
  • Copper was later mixed with tin, zinc and lead to form bronze.
  • In North India, chalcolithic age appeared after lithic age → then Iron Age.
  • In South India, lithic age directly shifted to Iron Age (no copper age).
  • Chalcolithic extension existed only in North India.

Stages of Chalcolithic Age

  1. Pre-Harappan Age (3500 BC – 2500 BC)
    Transitional phase between Neolithic and Harappan age.
  2. Harappan Age (2500 BC – 1750 BC)
    • Harappan civilisation was a Copper Age civilisation.
    • Other proposed names: Indus civilisation, Indus–Saraswati civilisation.
    • Most suitable name → Harappan Civilisation (Harappa was first to be discovered).
  3. Post-Harappan Age (2000 BC – 1000 BC)
    • Not an urban culture like Indus, but rural cultures.
    • Nomenclature based on type-site.
