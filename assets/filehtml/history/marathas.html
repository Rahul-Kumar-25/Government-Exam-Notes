History

Marathas Empire

MARATHAS (1674 - 1720)

Background

  • The marathas state emerged in the 17th century in western India (deccan)
  • It arose -
    • weakening of mughal control in the deccan
    • oppressive Mughal Revenue system
    • strong local warrior tradition
  • founder - "Shatrapati Shivaji Maharaj"

Rulers :

1. Shivaji (1674 - 1680)

  • Born in 1630 at Shivagni fort
  • Father "Shahji Bhonsle"
  • mother "Jija Bhai"
  • Religious teacher Samarth Ramadas
  • Guardian - "Dadaji Konda Deva" (Guru)
  • Title - "Shatrapati"
  • core language is Marathi & Sanskrit
  • coronated at capital Raigadh & Assume the title of Haindava Dharmodharak (Protector of Hinduism)

Administration of Shivaji :

  • central administration
  • Known as "Ashtapradham mandal" (council of 8 ministers)

8 ministers

  1. Peshwa (P.M)
  2. Amatya (finance minister)
  3. Sachiv (Head of Royal correspondence)
  4. mantri (Home minister)
  5. senapati (Army minister)
  6. Sumant (Foreign Affairs)
  7. Nyayadhish (Justice)
  8. Pandit Rao (Religious matters)

Note: minister were appointed not Heridatory

Provincial Administration:

  • Empire divided into Prants and prants into Parganas or Tarafs
  • Officials were paid cash salaries not "Jagir"

Revenue System

  • Land measurement using "Kathi"
  • Based on malik Ambari's system
  • Revenue demand 1/3 to 2/3rd of produce
  • No intermediators like "zamindar"

Taxes

  • Chauth - 25% tax from neighbouring territories
  • Sardeshmukhi i.e. 10% extra tax

Note: These Became major sources of income later

military System

  • standing Army
  • Soldiers were recruited directly by the state
  • No "Jagir" in the Army (system)

Army Structure

  • Army includes infantry, cavalry & Navy.

Note: "First strong Indian Navy"

  • Protected Konkan coast from "Portogese" & "Siddis"
  • Ports - one is Sindhu Durg & Vijay Durg

Fort Administration -

  • over 300 forts, Each fort had Killedar Sabnis & Samabat
  • Officers belong to different caste to prevent Rebellion.

Religious Policy:

  • Secular
  • Respected all Religion
  • Strict action against Forced conversion & Dishonouring women
  • Protected Mosque & Quran

Maratha mughal Relations:

  • conflict with Aurangzeb
  • Key Events -
    1. Treaty of Purandar (1665) - with Raja Jai Singh
    2. Escape from Agra (1666)

Successors of Shivaji

  1. Sambaji (1681 - 1689)
    • Son of Shivaji
    • captured & executed by Aurangzeb
    • Brave but less diplomatic
  2. Raja Ram (1689 - 1700)
    • He created the new post of "Pratinidhi"
    • taking the total number of 8 ministers to 9 minister.
  3. Tara Bai (1700 - 1707)
  4. Shahu (1707 - 1749)

Rise of Peshwas

  • After Shahus release Peshwas became powerful
  • Important Peshwas (1713 - 1720)

(I) Balaji Vishwanatha

  • made Peshwa Heridatory

(II) Baji Rao I (1720 - 1740)

  • Never lost the Battle
  • Expansion in North India

(III) Balaji Baji Rao (1740 - 1761)

  • popularly known as "Nana sahib"
  • Third battle of Panipat (1761) (B/W Peshwa & Abdali, Peshwa lost)

Successors of Balaji Baji Rao

  1. Madhav Rao (1761 - 1772)
  2. Narayana Rao (1772 - 73)
  3. Sawai Madhav Rao (1773 - 95)
  4. Baji Rao II (1795 - 1818)

Decline of marathas state :

Reasons:

  • internal conflicts
  • weak successors
  • Rise to British Power
  • Anglo marathas wars
    • first (1775 - 82)
    • second (1803 - 1805)
    • third (1817 - 1818) → End of marathas sovereignty
