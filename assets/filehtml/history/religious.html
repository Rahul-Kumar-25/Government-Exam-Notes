Religious Movements in India

Religious movements in India (15th cen. to 16th cen)

  • The 15th - 16th cen. in India witnessed powerful religious & social Reform (movements) that challenges rigid rituals, caste Hierarchy and religious in tolerance. The two most important movements were -
    1. Bhakti movement
    2. Sufi movement

Note: A major new Religious Sikhism was emerged in this period.

Background & cause

(i) Social conditions:

  • Rigid caste system
  • Dominence of Brahmanical Rituals
  • Exploitation by Priest & Religious elites
  • marginalization of lower caste & women

(ii) Religious conditions:

  • excessive ritualism in Hinduism
  • Orthodoxy in Islam
  • Growing Hindu, Muslim Tension

(iii) political conditions:

  • Establishment of Delhi Sultanate & Early Mughal rule
  • interaction b/w Hindu & Islam culture

Note: These conditions encouraged Reforms to promote simple devotion, equality & Humanism.

Bhakti movement

meaning:

  • Bhagti means "Devotion to God"
  • emphasised Personal devotion rather than rituals or Priestly mediation.

main feature:

  • Belief in one Supreme God
  • Rejection to caste discrimination
  • opposition to idol worship & complete Rituals
  • Emphasis on "love", "devotion" & "Surrender"
  • Use of Regional language instead of Sanskrit
  • Equality of all devotees including women & lower caste

forms of Bhakti:

(i) Saguna Bhakti:
  • worship of God with form & attributes
  • Example - Ram, krishan etc.
(ii) Nirguna Bhakti:
  • worship of formless God
  • Strong opposition to Rituals & idol worship

Early Bhakti Saints (In South India)

  1. Alvar
    • devotes of Vishnu
  2. Nayanars
    • devotes of Shiva

Bhakti movement (In North India)

  1. Kabir (1440 - 1518)
    • Born to a muslim weaver family
    • Criticised Both Hindu & muslim orthodoxy
    • Rejected Idol worship & Rituals
    • Preached unity of God
    • His teachings are in "Dohas"
  2. Guru Nanak (1469 - 1539)
    • founder of Sikhism
    • Believed in one formless God (Nirguna)
    • Rejected caste, rituals & priesthood
    • Emphasized
      • Naam Jap (Remembering God)
      • Kirat Karni (Honest living)
      • Vand Chakna (Sharing with other)
  3. Meera Bai (1498 - 1546)
    • Rajput Princess devoted to Lord Krishna
    • Her bhajan express intense devotion
    • Defied Social Norms & patriarchy
  4. Surdas (1483 - 1563)
    • Devoted of Lord krishna
    • wrote Sursagar
    • focused on krishnas childhood
  5. Tulsidas (1532 - 1623)
    • Author of Ramcharitmanas
    • promoted devotion to "lord Ram"
    • wrote in Avadhi

Others are:

  1. Ramananda (14-15 cen.)
  2. chaitanya - founder of Gaudiya or Bengal vaishnavism
  3. Vidyapati
  4. Purandara das
  5. Vallabhacharya
  6. Shankar deva
  7. Dadu dayal - founder of "dadupanth"
  8. Thyagaraja

Sufi movement

meaning:

  • Sufism is the mythical (Related to Atma) Branch of Islam
  • focused on inner purification & love for God

main feature:

  • Believe in one God
  • Rejection of Religious formalism
  • Emphasis on love, tolerance & service
  • Use of music to attain spiritual ecstasy
  • Respect for all Religions
  • Lived simple life

Major sufi orders in India

(i) Chishti Order:
  • founder in India - "Khawaja Moinuddin Chishti"
  • centres at Ajmer
  • Emphasis love, charity & tolerance
  • Avoided Association with Rulers
(ii) Suhra wardi Order
  • more orthodox
  • Accepted Royal Patronage
  • Active in Punjab & sind
(iii) Naqshbandi order
  • supported Shariya law
  • later influenced mughal Rulers

Important sufi saints

  1. Khawaja Moinuddin Chishti
  2. Nizamuddin Auliya (got title of mehboob-e-ilahi)
  3. Shaikh Salim Chishti

Sikhism

Origin

  • founded by "Guru nanak dev ji"
  • developed further by 9 "guru's"
  • consolidated during "Guru Gobind Singh" times

Key Beliefs

  1. Monotheism
  2. Equality of all Humans
  3. Condemnation of caste system
  4. Community Service i.e. langar

Religious Tax

  • Guru Granth Sahib

Impact of Religious Movement:

(i) Social Impact:

  • reduce caste Rigidity
  • improved status of women
  • encouraged social Harmony

(ii) Religious Impact:

  • Promoted Religious tolerance
  • Reduced Ritualism
  • Hinduism & Islam closer brought

(iii) Cultural Impact

  • Growth of Regional language
  • Development of devotional music & poetry

(iv) Political Impact

  • Influenced mughal Rulers especially Akbar's policy of tolerance
