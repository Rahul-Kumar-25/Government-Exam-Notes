Sangam Age

Sangam Age (300 BC to 300 AD)

Meaning of Sangam:

  1. Sangam means assembly or association of poets or scholars
  2. flourished in TN and adjoining regions
  3. Patronised mainly by Pandya kings
  4. Sangam literature is the earliest source of South Indian History

Sangam Assemblies:

There were three Sangams (acc. to traditions)

Sangam Location Fate
1st Sangam Madurai Destroyed by floods
2nd Sangam Kapatapuram Destroyed by floods
3rd Sangam Madurai Surviving literature

Sources of Sangam Age:

1. Literary Source:

a) Eight Anthologies → Ettuthogai

  1. Akananuru
  2. Purananuru
  3. Natrinai
  4. Kurunthogai
  5. Aingurunuru
  6. Kalithogai
  7. Paripadal
  8. Pathitrupathu

b) Pathuppattu

c) Tolkappiyam : Oldest Tamil Grammar written by Tolkappiyar

  • deals with Grammar, poetry and social life

d) Silappadikaram : written by Ilango Adigal

  • Epic story of Kannagi

e) Manimekalai : written by Sattanar

  • Buddhist influence

2. Archaeological Sources:

  • Roman coins
  • mega lithic burials
  • Ports like Arikamedu
  • Inscriptions (Tamil Brahmi)

Political History:

Major Dynasties (Muventar):

1. Cholas:

  • emblem – tiger
  • capital – Uraiyur
  • famous ruler – Karikala Chola
  • built Kallanai Dam (Grand Anicut)
  • They maintained an efficient Navy
  • They were wiped out in the attack of Pallavas from the North

2. Cheras:

  • emblem – bow
  • capital – Vanji
  • famous ruler – Senguttuvan
  • main ports – muziris and Tondi
  • promoted Paddini cult

3. Pandyas:

  • emblem – fish
  • capital – Madurai
  • Patronised Sangam literature
  • Earliest known Pandyan ruler was Mudukudimi
  • They find mention in Ramayan and Mahabharat
  • Greatest Pandya King Nendujelian

Administration:

  1. Monarchy
  2. King assisted by ministers
  3. Local assemblies like Ur i.e town, Nadu (mandalam) i.e province, Perur i.e Big village, Sirur i.e small village
  4. Army had infantry, cavalry, elephants, chariots
  5. Frequent wars for cattle raids

Society:

Social Structure:

Tinai → Region → occupation

  1. Kurinji → mountains → hunting
  2. Mullai → forest → pastoral
  3. Marutham → plains → Agriculture
  4. Neithal → coast → fishing
  5. Palai → Desert → Raiding

Status of women:

  1. Women enjoy respect
  2. Educated women poets existed
  3. Practice of Sati (rare)

Economy:

  1. Agriculture – main occupation
    • crops – Rice, sugarcane, cotton, pepper
    • for irrigation tanks and canals used
  2. Trade:
    • Extensive internal and foreign trade
    • Trade with Romans
    • Ports like Kaveri Pattanam, Arikamedu, Musiri
    • Roman Gold coins found

Religion:

  1. Nature worship
  2. Murugan (Tamil God most popular)
  3. Worship of Indra, Varuna, Koravai
  4. Jainism and Buddhism present
  5. No temple worship initially

Art & Culture:

  1. Hero stone worship (Nadokal)
  2. Music and Dance flourished
  3. Poetry highly developed
  4. Simple lifestyle

Sangam Polity:

  1. Thinai (Ecological Division)
  2. Karpu (Chastity)
  3. Nadokal (Hero stone)
  4. Puhar (Port Town)

Importance of Sangam Age:

  1. foundation of Tamil culture
  2. provides authentic history of South India
  3. shows Advanced social economic and political life
