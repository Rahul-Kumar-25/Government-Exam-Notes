Time Period: 30,00,000 BC to 1000 BC

Lithic Age is divided into three periods:

  • Neolithic Age or New Stone Age

Palaeolithic Age

Time Period: 30,00,000 BC to 10,000 BC

Discovery of palaeolithic archaeological material was made by Robert Bruce Foote in 1851. He discovered a palaeolithic implement Hand Axe in the district of Tamil Nadu (Chingleput district of Tamil Nadu).

R.B. Foote is known as the Father of Pre-historic Archaeology.

In 1982, Arun Sonakia discovered a woman‚Äôs skull from Hathnora, a place situated in the Narmada River valley, Hoshangabad district of Madhya Pradesh.

As per Arun Sonakia, this human skull belongs to the category of Homo erectus.

Palaeolithic Human Life

1. Food

  • Depended on hunting and nature-given things like meat, fruit or root
  • They did not know how to grow grains and vegetables
  • Their food was not cooked
  • Discovery of fire was accidental; striking together two pieces of quartzite stone produced a spark which, when it touched dry leaves, burst into a flame

2. Clothes

  • In rainy or winter season they wrapped animal skins, tree bark and large leaves around their body as clothes

3. Dwelling

  • They had no permanent dwelling places and lived as wanderers
  • They took shelter in caves, forests, leafy branches of large trees and under trees

4. Only Food Consumers

  • They were food gatherers
  • They largely depended on nature for food supply
  • They were consumers of food but not food producers

5. Disposal of Dead Person

  • Dead bodies were placed in open spaces to be eaten by birds and animals

On the basis of differences in stone implements, the Palaeolithic period is divided into three ages:

  • (i) Lower or Earlier Palaeolithic Age
  • (ii) Middle Palaeolithic Age
  • (iii) Upper or Later Palaeolithic Age

(i) Lower or Earlier Palaeolithic Age

  • People used quartzite and other stones to make tools, implements and weapons
  • Cultures of Lower Palaeolithic period are divided into:
    • Chopper‚ÄìChopping Pebble Culture (Sohan Culture, Pakistan) ‚Äì First found in Sohan River valley of West Punjab
    • Hand Axe Culture (Madras Culture) ‚Äì First found at Badmadurai of Madras

Main Implements:

  • Chopper
  • Chopping pebble
  • Hand axe
  • Cleaver
  • Scraper

(ii) Middle Palaeolithic Age

  • Main stone implements were flakes
  • Implements included scrapers, burins and borers
  • This age is also called Flake Culture
  • Good quality quartzite and precious stones like chert, jasper and flint were used

(iii) Upper or Late Palaeolithic Age

This period is marked by innovation in tools and technology. Parallel sided blades, burins and some instances of bone tools were used.
