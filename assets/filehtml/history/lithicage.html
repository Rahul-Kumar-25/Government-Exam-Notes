Lithic Age or Stone Age

Time Period: 30,00,000 BC to 1000 BC

Some archaeological evidences were found in which stone implements are in great amount, that‚Äôs why this period is called Lithic Age or Stone Age.

Lithic Age is divided into three periods:

  • Palaeolithic Age or Old Stone Age
  • Mesolithic Age or Middle Stone Age
  • Neolithic Age or New Stone Age

ùüô üìã Palaeolithic Age

Time Period: 30,00,000 BC to 10,000 BC

Discovery of palaeolithic archaeological material was made by Robert Bruce Foote in 1851. He discovered a palaeolithic implement Hand Axe in the district of Tamil Nadu (Chingleput district of Tamil Nadu).

R.B. Foote is known as the Father of Pre-historic Archaeology.

In 1982, Arun Sonakia discovered a woman‚Äôs skull from Hathnora, a place situated in the Narmada River valley, Hoshangabad district of Madhya Pradesh.

As per Arun Sonakia, this human skull belongs to the category of Homo erectus.

Palaeolithic Human Life

1. Food

  • Depended on hunting and nature-given things like meat, fruit or root
  • They did not know how to grow grains and vegetables
  • Their food was not cooked
  • Discovery of fire was accidental; striking together two pieces of quartzite stone produced a spark which, when it touched dry leaves, burst into a flame

2. Clothes

  • In rainy or winter season they wrapped animal skins, tree bark and large leaves around their body as clothes

3. Dwelling

  • They had no permanent dwelling places and lived as wanderers
  • They took shelter in caves, forests, leafy branches of large trees and under trees

4. Only Food Consumers

  • They were food gatherers
  • They largely depended on nature for food supply
  • They were consumers of food but not food producers

5. Disposal of Dead Person

  • Dead bodies were placed in open spaces to be eaten by birds and animals

üõ†Ô∏èPalaeolithic Implements or Toolsüõ†Ô∏è

On the basis of differences in stone implements, the Palaeolithic period is divided into three ages:

  • (i) Lower or Earlier Palaeolithic Age
  • (ii) Middle Palaeolithic Age
  • (iii) Upper or Later Palaeolithic Age

(i) Lower or Earlier Palaeolithic Age

  • People used quartzite and other stones to make tools, implements and weapons
  • Cultures of Lower Palaeolithic period are divided into:
    • Chopper‚ÄìChopping Pebble Culture (Sohan Culture, Pakistan) ‚Äì First found in Sohan River valley of West Punjab
    • Hand Axe Culture (Madras Culture) ‚Äì First found at Badmadurai of Madras

Main Implements:

  • Chopper
  • Chopping pebble
  • Hand axe
  • Cleaver
  • Scraper

(ii) Middle Palaeolithic Age

  • Main stone implements were flakes
  • Implements included scrapers, burins and borers
  • This age is also called Flake Culture
  • Good quality quartzite and precious stones like chert, jasper and flint were used

(iii) Upper or Late Palaeolithic Age

This period is marked by innovation in tools and technology. Parallel sided blades, burins and some instances of bone tools were used.

ùüö üìã Mesolithic Age

(9000 BC to 4000 BC)

This was the age of transition because they neither totally abandoned the characteristics of Palaeolithic age nor totally adopted the features of Neolithic age.

  • Discovery of mesolithic archaeological material ‚Üí John Evans
  • Excavated Sakhar and Rohri in 1866

Key Aspects of Mesolithic Age

  • Reduction in the size of well-established tool types
  • Characteristic tools were microliths made of:
    • Crypto-crystalline silica
    • Chalcedony
    • Chert

Mesolithic Human Life

  1. Taming of Animals
    Still depended largely on hunting, but now they began domesticating animals like:
    • Dog
    • Sheep
    • Sheep-goat
    • Cow-ox
    • Buffalo
    • Wild horse etc.
  2. Burial of the Dead
    They buried their dead in graves and placed food stuff, tools, weapons etc. as grave goods. Probably they believed in life after death.
  3. Shelter
    They lived in semi-permanent and temporary settlements along with occupying caves and open grounds.
  4. Painting
    Certain rock paintings give ideas about:
    • Development of religious practices
    • Division of labour on the basis of gender

    In 1867, first rock painting in India was discovered at Sohagighat (Kaimur hills, U.P.)

üõ†Ô∏èMesolithic Implementsüõ†Ô∏è

  • Agate, Jasper, Chert, Chalcedony etc. ‚Üí used as raw material
  • Bones and Horn

ùüë üìã Neolithic or New Stone Age

7000 BC to 1000 BC

The discovery of Neolithic archaeological material ‚Üí Dr. Primrose
He discovered neolithic implements, polished stone knives and arrow heads from Karnataka (Lingusur) in 1842.

Neolithic Human Life

Three main characteristics of this age were crop cultivation (agriculture), animal husbandry and fixed habitation.

Note: Mehrgarh (Paki Bolan District, Baluchistan Province, Pakistan) is called the earliest known village site of Indian subcontinent.

1. Agriculture

Men of this age became food producer as well as food consumer.

  • Main cultivated crops ‚Üí wheat, barley, paddy, millets and various types of vegetables
  • Earliest evidence of wheat and barley cultivation was found from Mehrgarh

Imp:

The oldest evidence of paddy cultivation was found from Koldihawa (Allahabad District, U.P.) in the Indian subcontinent as well as in the whole world.

2. Animal Husbandry

  • Animal husbandry became extended
  • The main animals were dog, sheep, goat, horse etc.
  • Main occupation were animal hunting, cattle catching then taming sheep-goat etc.
  • The nomadic life came to an end now and they used to live a life of farmer that settled in a place.

3. Clothes

Men started growing cotton and learnt about cloth.

  • The earliest evidence of cotton in the world was found from Mehrgarh
  • India is the first country in the world whose people learnt the art of spinning and weaving of cloths

4. Houses

Neolithic men lived in huts that had been made on the ground by bamboos and smearing of clay.

Exception: Kashmir Valley, Punjab (Pakistan)

In Kashmir Valley and Punjab (Pakistan) neolithic men developed dwelled in pits which is called pit-dwelling.

5. Pottery

The neolithic era communities first made pottery by hand and then with the help of potter‚Äôs wheel.

  • Their pottery included Black Burnished ware, grey ware and mat-impressed ware
  • Pottery on a large scale appear on this phase

6. Burial Process

a. North India

There were two types of burial process ‚Äî complete & partial burial process

  • In complete burial process the circular pit was digged first and after that in that pit the dead person was laid flat on the back
  • In partial process selected bones of the dead person were buried in the grave
  • The custom of burial of the pet animal dog with his master was practiced at Burzahom & Gufkral of Kashmir
  • Pet goat burial at Mehrgarh

b. South India

Two types of burial process i.e. complete & partial

  • Complete burial process ‚Üí the corpse of old male and female and small babies were buried completely in the grave
  • Partial burial process ‚Üí selected bones of the dead person were buried

üõ†Ô∏èNeolithic Implementsüõ†Ô∏è

  • Implements of this age are pecked, ground & polished
  • Implements were made from stones other than Quartzite stone
  • The main implements were celt, hatcher, chisel, pestle, arrow-head, saw etc.
  • Neolithic level has been accepted as metal less culture
