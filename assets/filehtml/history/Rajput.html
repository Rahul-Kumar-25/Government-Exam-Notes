History

Rajput Period North India

Rajput (6th century to 12th century AD) – North India

Origin of Rajput

  • The word Rajput comes from Rajputra
  • Emerged as a political and military class after the decline of Gupta Empire.
  • Many historians believe Rajputs have mixed origins
    • Ancient Kshatriyas
    • foreign tribes
    • local ruling clans

Agnikula Theory

Acc. to this theory some Rajputs originated from a sacrificial fire (Agnikund) at mount Abu

4 Agnikula Rajput clans:

  1. Parthihar
  2. Parmar
  3. Solanki (Chalukyas)
  4. Chohanas

Major Rajput Dynasties

1. Gurjara Pratihara

  • capital – Kannauj
  • important rulers:
    • Nagabhatta - I – resisted Arab invasion
    • Mihir bhoja – greatest ruler
  • dominated north India in 8th to 10th century
  • played key role in Tripartite struggle
  • Note: Known as barrier against Arab invasion

2. Palas

  • Region – Bihar and Bengal
  • Founder – Gopala
  • Famous ruler – Dharmpala
  • Patron of Buddhism
  • Nalanda and Vikramshila University flourished

3. Rashtrakutas

  • Mainly Deccan based, they intervened in North India
  • Rulers – Dhruv and Govind - III
  • Part of Tripartite struggle

4. Chohans (Chahamanas)

  • Region – Ajmer & Delhi
  • capital – Ajmer
  • famous ruler – Prithviraj Chohan (battle with mohammad gori)
  • Battles:
    • First battle of Tarain defeated mohammad gori
    • Second battle of Tarain defeated and killed
  • Note: Second battle of Tarain marks end of major Rajput resistance

5. Parmaras

  • Region – Malwa
  • capital – Dhar
  • famous ruler – Bhoja
  • Patron of Sanskrit learning and architecture

6. Solanki (Chalukyas)

  • Region – Gujarat
  • capital – Anhilwara
  • Known for temple architecture (Sun Temple)

Tripartite Struggle

  • Conflict for control over Kannauj
  • between Pratiharas, Palas and Rashtrakutas
  • Significance:
    • made India vulnerable to foreign invasions
    • prevented political unity in North India
    • weakened all three powers

Political System of Rajputs (Samanta System)

  1. Feudal system – king was supreme but dependent on feudatories
  2. Frequent wars among Rajput clans
  3. Lack of political unity
  4. Weakness:
    • disunity
    • no standing army
    • failure to adapt to new war fare methods
    • personal valor over strategies

Society

  1. Caste system rigid
  2. Rajputs were warriors and land owners
  3. Position of women:
    • practice of Sati
    • child marriage
    • Jauhar during invasion
    • women respected but socially restricted

Economy

  1. Agriculture was main occupation
  2. Land grants to Brahmanas and temples
  3. Trade declined compare to Gupta period
  4. Coins were issued but less standardized

Religion

  1. Hinduism dominant (Shaivism and Vaishnavism)
  2. Buddhism declined (except under Palas)
  3. Jainism patronised by some rulers

Art and Architecture

  1. Nagara system dominant
  2. Examples:
    • Sun temple Modhera
    • Khajuraho Temple
    • Dilwara Jain temple
  3. Literature:
    • Sanskrit flourished
    • Court poet patronised
    • Works on Astronomy, medicine and philosophy

Battle with Turks

  1. Early resistance against Mahmud of Ghazni and Mahmud Gori
  2. Rajput fought bravely but lack unity
  3. Defeat at 2nd battle of Tarain opened the door for Delhi Sultanat

Causes of Decline

  1. internal conflicts
  2. feudal fragmentation
  3. lack of political unity
  4. outdated military tactics
  5. Repeated foreign invasions
