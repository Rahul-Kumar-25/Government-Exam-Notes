History

Post Gupta Period

Post Gupta (550 to 647 AD)

  • It is transition from Ancient to medieval India
  • Period after the decline of Gupta Empire
  • Marked by political fragmentation, rise of regional kingdoms and feudal tendencies

Major Sources

  1. Literary Sources:
    • Harshacharita – Banabhatta
    • Account of Chinese traveller Hiuen tsang
    • Puranas
    • Religious text (like buddhist and Brahmanical)
  2. Archaeological Sources:
    • inscriptions
    • copper plate grants
    • seals and coins

Major Kingdom and Dynasties

Pushyabhuti or Vardhana Dynasty

  • First ruler → Prabhakaravardhana (580 to 605 AD)
    • strengthen kingdom
  • Next ruler → Harshavardhana (606 - 647 AD)
    • capital – Thanesar and later Kannauj
    • Expanded empire over North India
    • defeated by Pulakesin - II on Narmada (Chalukya king)
    • converted to Mahayana buddhism
    • organized religious assemblies
    • issued very few coins
    • Not a conqueror like Guptas but a unifier

Administration under Harsha

  1. monarchy
  2. provinces called Bhuktis
  3. official paid mostly through land grants
  4. Army included infantry, cavalry, elephants etc.

Cultural Contributions

  1. Nalanda university patronised
  2. Literary work by Harsha like Ratnavali, Nagananda, Priyadarsika etc.

Chinese traveller Hiuen tsang

  1. Visited during Harsha's reign
  2. spend time at Nalanda
  3. wrote Si-yu-ki
  4. Described social conditions, buddhist institutions and Harsha's administration

Regional Kingdom in Post Gupta Period

Chalukyas of Badami

  1. capital – Vatapi (Badami)
  2. famous ruler – Pulakesin - II
  3. defeated Harshavardhana
  4. patronised art and architecture
  5. Rock cut caves at Badami
  6. Aihole inscription by his court poet Ravi Kirti
  7. Pallava ruler Narasimham varman mammar killed Pulakesin - II and captured Vatapi (vadami) and adopted the title vatapikonda

Pallavas

  1. capital – Kanchipuram
  2. famous ruler like Mahendra varman - I and Narasimha varman (vatapi konda)
  3. Developed Dravidian temple architecture
  4. Rock cut temple at Mahabalipuram

Rashtrakutas

  1. capital: manyakheta
  2. famous ruler: Amoghavarsha
  3. Patron of literature
  4. Kailasa temple at Ellora

Administration of Post Gupta

  1. land grants to Brahmanas, officials, religious institutions, etc.
  2. Rise of Samantas
  3. weak central authority
  4. Decline in Urban centres

Economy

  1. Agriculture dominated
  2. Decline in trade and commerce
  3. fewer gold coins
  4. Growth of self sufficient villages

Society

  1. Varnas system become rigid.
  2. Brahmanas enjoyed privileges
  3. Position of women declined
  4. Practices like sati and child marriage spread

Religion

  1. Decline of Buddhism
  2. Revival of Brahmanism
  3. Growth of Shaivism and Vaishnavism
  4. Bhakti elements emerged

Art & Architecture

  1. Temple architecture flourished
  2. shift from Buddhist to Hindu monuments
  3. Early development of Nagara style (North India) and Dravida style (South India)

Decline of Post Gupta Polities

  1. continuous wars
  2. weak successors
  3. economic decline
  4. Rise of new regional powers like Pallavas and Rashtrakutas
