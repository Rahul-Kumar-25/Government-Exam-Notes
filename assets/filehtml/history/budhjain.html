Buddhism & Jainism

Founder of Buddhism:

Gautam Buddha AKA Siddhartha Gautama

  • Born in 563 BC
  • Born at Lumbini (Nepal)
  • Father was king Suddhodana (chief of Shakya clan)
  • Mother was queen Mahamaya

Early life:

  • He lived a luxurious life as a prince
  • married to Yashodhara
  • son – Rahula

Renunciation:

  • left home at age 29
  • 6 साल साधना करने के बाद समझा
  • Realised that extreme austerity is not the solution

Enlightenment (Nirvana):

  • Bodh Gaya में ज्ञान प्राप्त (Bihar)
  • under the Bodhi tree
  • at age of 35
  • became Buddha the enlightened one

First Sermon:

  • delivered at Sarnath near Varanasi
  • known as धर्म चक्र प्रवर्तन
  • founded the Buddhist संघ

Death (Mahaparinirvana):

  • died at age of 80
  • at Kushinagar (UP)
  • year – 483 BC

Core teachings of Buddhism:

Four noble truth:

  • (i) दुःख → life is full of suffering
  • (ii) तृष्णा → desire is the cause of suffering
  • (iii) निरोध → suffering can be ended
  • (iv) मार्ग → path leading to end of suffering

8 fold path (अष्टांगिक मार्ग):

  • (i) Right Vision
  • (ii) Right thought or attitude
  • (iii) Right or whole speech
  • (iv) right or integral action – don’t commit violence
  • don’t covet property of others
  • don’t indulge in corrupt practices
  • don’t speak lie
  • don’t use intoxicants
  • (v) right livelihood or proper livelihood
  • (vi) right effort or energy
  • (vii) Right mindfulness
  • (viii) Right concentration or meditation

middle path (मध्यम मार्ग):

  • (i) Avoid extreme luxury
  • (ii) Avoid extreme austerity
  • (iii) emphasize balance

Important concept:

  • (i) कर्म – action determine future
  • (ii) पुनर्जन्म – cycle of birth & death
  • (iii) निर्वाण – liberation from rebirth
  • (iv) अहिंसा – non violence
  • (v) त्रिरत्न – three jewels
    • a. Buddha
    • b. Dharma
    • c. Sangha

Buddhist council:

Place King Key outcome
Rajgriha Ajatashatru compilation of teachings
Vaishali Kalasoka split begins
Pataliputra Ashoka spread of Buddhism
Kashmir Kanishka Mahayana rise, Hinayana rise

Buddhist sect:

  • Hinayana → original teachings
  • Buddha as teacher
  • Nirvana through self effort
  • Mahayana → Buddha as divine
  • Bodhisattva concept
  • idol worship
  • Vajrayana → use of mantras & rituals
  • developed later

Buddhist literature:

  • written in Pali
  • Tripitaka (three basket)
    • Vinaya Pitaka → rules for monks
    • Sutta Pitaka → teachings of Buddha
    • Abhidhamma Pitaka → philosophy

language of teachings: Pali and Prakrit

oppose Sanskrit dominance

Symbols of Buddhism:

  • Lotus & bull – birth
  • Horse – renunciation
  • Bodhi tree – enlightenment
  • Foot print – nirvana

Role of Ashoka:

  • Adopted Buddhism after Kalinga war
  • Spread Buddhism to Sri Lanka, Central Asia, South east Asia
  • Built stupas and viharas

Reason for popularity:

  • simple language
  • no caste system
  • equality
  • emphasis on morality

Decline of Buddhism:

  • revival of Brahmanism
  • complex rituals in Mahayana
  • loss of royal support
  • invasions

Jainism

Origin & background:

  • Jainism is one of the oldest indian religions
  • reject authority of vedas, caste system, animal sacrifice
  • emphasise self discipline, non violence, and liberation of the soul

Tirthankaras:

means ford maker (one who shows the path to salvation)

total 24 tirthankaras

  1. Rishabhnath (Adhinath) – symbol Bull
  2. Ajitnath – Elephant
  3. Sambhavnath – Horse
  4. Abhinandan – Monkey
  5. Sumatinath – Curlew
  6. Padmaprabhu – Red lotus
  7. Suparshvanath – Swastik
  8. Chandraprabhu – Moon
  9. Suvidhinath – Crocodile
  10. Shitalnath – Shrivatsa
  11. Shreyansnath – Rhinoceros
  12. Vasupujya – Buffalo
  13. Vimalnath – Boar
  14. Anantnath – Falcon
  15. Dharmanath – Vajra
  16. Shantinath – Deer
  17. Kunthunath – He-goat
  18. Aranath – Fish
  19. Mallinath – Water pot
  20. Munisuvrata – Tortoise
  21. Neminath – Blue lotus
  22. Arishtanemi – Conch shell
  23. Parshwanath – Symbol Snake
  24. Mahavira – Symbol Lion

Parshwanath:

  • lived around 8th century BC
  • preist four vows

Mahavira:

  • Born 599 BC – Kundagrama (Vaishali, Bihar)
  • Jnatrika clan
  • father was Siddhartha
  • mother was Trishala
  • attained Kaivalya at age of 42
  • died 527 BC – Pavapuri (Bihar)

Mahavira’s teaching:

He added fifth vow to Parshwanath four vows

5 Great vows (पंचमहाव्रत):

  1. अहिंसा
  2. सत्य
  3. अस्तेय (non stealing)
  4. ब्रह्मचर्य – added by mahavir
  5. अपरिग्रह

Philosophy of Jainism:

  • Tri-ratna:
    • Samyak Darshana – Right faith
    • Samyak Gyana – Right knowledge
    • Samyak Charitra – Right conduct

Concept of Soul: Every living being has a soul

Even plant, water, air, fire possesses life

Karma Theory: Karma is material substance that binds to the soul

Liberation comes by removing karma through right conduct

Austerity and meditation

Sectarian Divisions:

Jainism splits around 300 BC

  • Digambara
    • Sky-clad (no clothes)
    • believe women cannot attain liberation
    • mahavira lived naked
    • monks practice extreme austerity
  • Shvetambar
    • White-clad
    • believe women can attain liberation
    • scriptures preserved in written form

Jain Council:

  • First council – Pataliputra – 300 BC – compilation of teachings
  • Second council – Vallabhi – 5th century – final compilation (Shvetambar text)

Jain literature:

  • written in Prakrit
  • Jain scriptures are called Agamas
  • Important text: Angas (core text), Upangas, Prakirnas, Kalpasutra (Biography of Mahavira)

Symbol of Jainism:

  • Ahimsa hand with wheel
  • Swastika – forms of existence
  • Jain flag – 5 colors representing Panch Mahavrat

Jain Art and Architecture:

  • Emphasis on idol worship
  • Notable sculptures:
    • Dilwara temple (Mount Abu)
    • Gomateshwara statue (Shravanabelagola)
  • Patronage by Mauryas, Guptas and Rashtrakutas
