History

Ancient - Vedic Culture

Vedic Culture

  • Some scholars believe that Aryans were native to the soil of India and some other scholars believe that Aryans were migrated from outside (Europe, Central Asia, Arctic region, Tibet etc.).
  • It is also believed that the earliest Aryans lived in the land of Sapta Sindhu i.e. seven rivers
    • Indus (Sindhu)
    • Jhelum
    • Beas
    • Chenab
    • Ravi
    • Sutlej
    • Saraswati (modern Ghaggar Hakra)

Vedic Literature (1500 BC – 600 BC)

  • Rig Veda was composed while the Aryans were still in the Punjab.
  • Vedic literature comprises four literary productions:
    • Samhitas / Vedas
    • Brahmanas
    • Aranyakas
    • Upanishads
  • Vedic literature was handed down from generation to generation by word of mouth. These are called Shruti (to hear).
  • Vedas are called Apaurusheya (not created by men but God gifted) and Nitya (existing in all eternity).

Vedas

There are four Vedas:

  1. Rig Veda
  2. Yajur Veda
  3. Sama Veda
  4. Atharva Veda

The first three Vedas are jointly called Vedatrayi.

Rig Veda

  • Oldest text in the world.
  • Contains 1028 hymns and 10580 verses.
  • Divided into 10 Mandalas.
  • Mandala 2–7 are called Gotra or Vamsha Mandalas.
  • First and tenth mandalas were added later.
  • Contains Purushasukta explaining four varnas (Brahmana, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra).
  • Recited by Hotri.

Yajur Veda

  • Deals with procedures for sacrifices.
  • Recited by Adhvaryu.
  • Divided into:
    • Krishna Yajur Veda (Black Yajur Veda)
    • Shukla Yajur Veda (White Yajur Veda / Vajasaneya)
  • Written in verse and prose.
  • Shukla Yajur Veda contains only mantras.

Sama Veda

  • Verses mostly taken from Rig Veda and arranged for singing.
  • Collection of 1810 melodies.
  • Contains famous Dhrupada Raga.
  • Contains 1549 verses.
  • Recited by Udgatri.

Atharva Veda

  • Collection of magic spells and charms.
  • Used to ward off evil spirits and diseases.
  • Considered non-Aryan work.
  • Not included in Vedas for a long time.

Brahmanas

  • Describe rules for sacrificial ceremonies.
  • Written in prose and ritualistic in nature.
  • Attached Brahmanas:
    • Rig Veda – Aitareya, Kaushitiki
    • Yajur Veda – Shatapatha (oldest & largest), Taittiriya
    • Sama Veda – Panchavimsha, Shadvimsha, Chandogya, Jaiminiya
    • Atharva Veda – Gopatha

Aranyakas

  • Called Forest Books.
  • Written for hermits and students living in forests.
  • Concluding part of Brahmanas.
  • Interpret rituals philosophically and oppose sacrifices.

Upanishads

  • Literal meaning: to sit near someone.
  • Deal with philosophy of life, universe, self and soul.
  • Total 108 Upanishads.
  • Focus on Atman and Brahman.
  • Known as Vedanta.
  • Mandukya Upanishad is the largest.
  • Brihadaranyaka Upanishad is the oldest.

Literature of Vedic Tradition (600 BC – 600 AD)

  1. Vedangas / Sutras
  2. Smritis / Dharmashastras
  3. Mahakavya (Epics)
  4. Puranas
  5. Upavedas
  6. Shad Darshanas

Note: This is Smriti (Remembrance literature).

Vedangas

  • Means limbs of Vedas.
  • Supplementary texts for understanding Vedas.
  • Human origin, not Shruti.
  • Written in Sutra form.
  • Six Vedangas:
    • Shiksha
    • Kalpa Sutras
    • Jyotisha
    • Vyakarana
    • Nirukta – oldest dictionary
    • Chhanda
  • Shiksha – Pratishakhya is oldest text
  • Vyakarana – Ashtadhyayi is oldest
  • Chhanda – Pingala text
  • Jyotisha – Lagadha Muni
  • Kalpa Sutras:
    • Shrauta Sutras – Vedic sacrifices
    • Grihya Sutras – Family ceremonies
    • Dharma Sutras – Varnas & Ashrams
