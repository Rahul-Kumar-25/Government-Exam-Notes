History

Gupta Period

Gupta Period (320 AD – 550 AD)

  • Known as the golden age of the Indian ancient India

Sources of Gupta History:

  1. Literary Source:
    • Puranas
    • Allahabad Pillar Inscription → written by Harisena
    • work of Kalidasa like Raghuvamsha, Meghaduta, Abhijnanasakuntalam
    • Chinese Traveller Fa-Hien → described society during Chandragupta
  2. Archaeological Sources:
    • Gold coins, seals and inscriptions
    • iron pillar of Mehrauli

Important Gupta Rulers

  1. Sri Gupta – founder of Gupta Dynasty
  2. Ghatotkacha
  3. Chandragupta - I – first independent Rule (320 AD – 335 AD)
    • Adopted title Maharajadhiraja
    • married Kumara Devi (Lichchhavi Princess)
    • issued gold coins (Dinars)
  4. Samudragupta (335 – 375 AD)
    • Napoleon of India
    • Allahabad Pillar inscription sources
    • famous for military conquests
    • Policy of Annexation and tributary states
    • Also a great musician and poet
    • Performed Ashvamedha Yajna
  5. Chandragupta - II (375 – 415 AD)
    • AKA – Vikramaditya
    • most powerful Gupta ruler
    • defeated Sakas kshatrapas
    • capital Pataliputra
    • Court of Navratnas (Nine gems)
      • like Kalidas, Varahamihira, Amarasimha, Dhanvantari
  6. Kumara Gupta - I (415 AD – 455 AD)
    • founded Nalanda University
    • introduced Kartikeya worship
  7. Skandagupta (455 – 467 AD)
    • last great Gupta ruler
    • repelled Huna invasion
    • Gupta Decline started after him

Administration:

  1. Monarchy
  2. Empire divided into:
    • Bhuktis means province headed by Uparika or Bhogapati
    • Vishaya i.e. District headed by Vishayapati or Ayukta
    • Nagar or Vithika i.e city headed by Nagarapati or Purapala
    • Gram i.e. village headed by gramika
  3. Land grant to Brahmanas and Temples (Agrahara)
  4. Local bodies existed

Economy:

  1. Agriculture – main occupation
    • land grant reduced state revenue
  2. Trade and commerce:
    • internal & foreign trade
    • decline of Roman Trade later
    • guilds existed
  3. Coins:
    • large no. of Gold coins
    • indicated prosperity

Society:

  1. Brahmanas gains dominance
  2. Practice of untouchability appears
  3. Practice of sati (first example)
  4. Women were denied any property right.
  5. position of sudras seems to have improved
  6. Early marriages were advocated
  7. Varna system begins to get rigid

Religion:

  1. Brahmanism Revived
  2. Growth of Vaishnavism and Shaivism
  3. Gupta rulers were vaishnava
  4. Buddhism and Jainism continued.

Art and Architecture:

  1. Sculpture: Buddha statue at Sarnath
  2. Development of Nagaram style – Architecture
  3. Temple like Dashavatara temple – Deogadh and Bhitargaon temple
  4. first time we get image of vishnu, shiva and other Gods.
  5. Rock cut caves found like Ajanta and Ellora
  6. Paintings are found in Bagh (MP) and Ajanta cave (MH)

Science and Literature:

Science:

  1. Aryabhat – Aryabhatiya, rotation of Earth and value of π
  2. Varahamihira – Brihat Samhita
  3. Charaka and Sushruta – medicine and surgery

Literature:

  1. Sanskrit literature flourished
  2. Amarasimha – Amarakosha
  3. Kalidas – greatest Sanskrit poet.

Decline of Gupta Empire:

  1. weak successors
  2. huna invasion
  3. land grant weakened economy
  4. Rise of regional powers
  5. Rising world distinction and social disorder
  6. Development of feudal elements (rising land grants)

Why Gupta Age is called Golden Age

favour:

  1. Political stability
  2. Economic prosperity
  3. Advanced in science & Art
  4. Classical Sanskrit literature
  5. Revival of Hinduism (secular)
  6. Great personage like Kalidas, Amar Simha, Aryabhat etc.
  7. Mild punishment and taxes were light

Against:

  1. decline in status of women
  2. decline of urban centres
  3. decline of trade
  4. Absence of large central army and bureaucracy.
  5. Increasing world distinction and social disorder
  6. Development of feudal elements (rising land grants)
