Polity

Supreme Court

Supreme Court of India

Common Feature

Single system of courts adopted from Government of India Act, 1935 (Unlike USA).

Constitutional Provisions

  • Articles 124–147 (Part V)
  • Replaced the Privy Council as the highest court of appeal
  • Inaugurated on 28 January 1950
  • Succeeded the Federal Court of India (established under GOI Act, 1935)

Strength of Judges

  • Originally: 8 (1 Chief Justice + 7 Judges)
  • Present: 34 (1 Chief Justice + 33 Judges)

Increase in Number of Judges

  • 1950 – 7
  • 1956 – 10
  • 1960 – 13
  • 1977 – 17
  • 1986 – 25
  • 2009 – 30
  • 2019 – 33

Appointment

  • Judges appointed by President
  • Chief Justice of India appointed by President after consultation with Supreme Court & High Court judges
  • Other judges appointed by President after consultation with Chief Justice of India & High Court judges

Note: No Minimum age or fixed period is prescribed for appointment of judges in Supreme Court.

Qualifications

  • Citizen of India
  • Not above 65 years of age
  • High Court Judge for at least 5 years OR
  • Advocate of High Court for at least 10 years

Tenure

  • Retirement age – 65 years
  • Can resign to President
  • Can be removed by President
  • Removal grounds: Proven misbehavior or incapacity

Procedure of Removal (Impeachment)

  • Motion signed by:
    • 100 members in Lok Sabha OR
    • 50 members in Rajya Sabha
  • Given to Speaker/Chairman
  • Investigated by 3-member committee (2 SC judges + 1 distinguished jurist)
  • If found guilty → Motion taken for consideration
  • Passed by Special Majority:
    • Majority of total membership +
    • 2/3rd of members present & voting
  • President passes removal order

Jurisdiction

  • Original
  • Appellate
  • Advisory

Salary & Allowances

Paid from Consolidated Fund of India.

Language

English

Oath

Administered by President.
