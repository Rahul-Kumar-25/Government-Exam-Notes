Union / India And Its Union Territories

Part I    Article 1 to 4

Article 1

India i.e. Bharat

(India is a union of states mentioned)

Article 1 describes India i.e. Bharat as a “union of state” rather than “Federation of states”. Acc. to Dr. Ambedkar, the phrase “union of states” has been preferred to “Federation of states” for two reasons :-

(1) The Indian federation is not the result of an agreement among the states like the American federation.

(2) States have no right to secede the federation.

Article 1, the territory of India can be classified into three categories :-

(1) Territories of States

(2) Union Territories

(3) Territories that may be acquired by the govt. of India at anyone

The names of state and union territories and their territorial extent are mentioned in the first schedule of constitution.

Article 2

(Indian can make the new state outside India Have power)

Article 2 empowers the parliament to admit into union of states or establish new states on such terms & conditions as it thinks fit. Article 2 runs two power to the parliament

(1) Power to admit into the Union of India in new state.

(2) Power to establish new states.

Article 3

(Within state)

It authorises the parliament to

(1) Form a new state by separation of territory from any state or by uniting two or more states or parts of state or by uniting any territory to a part of any state

(2) Increase the area of any state

(3) Diminish the area of any state

(4) Alter the boundaries of any state

(5) Alter the name of any state

The above changes can be introduced in the parliament only with the prior recommendation of the president.

Note : The President has to refer the same to the state legislature concern for expressing its views within specified period.

Article 2 relates to the admission or establishment of new states that are not part of the “union of India”

Article 3 on the other hand relates to the formation of changes in the existing states of “union of India”

Article 4

Add / Citt – simple majority

Secede – Special majority

Article 368 C.A.

Article 4, itself declares that laws made for admission or establishment of new state (Article 2) and formation of new state & alternation of area, boundaries or names of existing states (Article 3) are not to be considered as amendment of the constitution (Article 368).

This means that such laws can be passed by the simple majority and by the ordinary legislative process.
