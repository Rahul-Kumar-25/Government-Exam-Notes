State - Governer

State

Governor

Tenure: 5 years (pleasure of President)

  • First citizen of state
  • Act as agent of central government
  • Elections → no election
  • Appointed by President

Qualification

  • Citizen of India
  • Age = 35 years
  • Must not hold office of profit

Conditions

  • Should not be the member of either house of LA or LC or member of parliament
  • Should not hold office of profit
  • Raj Bhavan

Allowance

  • Salary decided by Parliament
  • Cannot be diminished

Vacancy

  • Death
  • Resign
  • Tenure → 5 years (till President pleasure)
  • Removal → direct by President

Oath: Chief Justice of High Court

Powers

Administrative Powers

  • He/She can appoint Chief Minister and other ministers
  • Advocate General of State
  • State Election Commissioner
  • Chairman and members of State Public Service Commission (however they can be removed only by the President)
  • He can nominate rules for more convenient transaction of business of state government
  • He can seek any information relating to the administration of affairs of the state
  • He/She act as the Chancellor of University in the state
  • He/She can recommend the imposition of constitutional emergencies in a state to the President
  • He/She can require the Chief Minister to submit for the consideration of the Council of Ministers any matter on which decision has been taken by a minister

Legislative Powers

  • He/She can summon or prorogue the state legislature and dissolve the state legislative assembly
  • He/She can address the state legislature at the commencement of the first session after each general election and the first session of each year
  • He/She can send messages to the house or houses of state legislature with respect to bill pending in the legislature
  • Can nominate 1/6th of the members of State Legislative Council
  • Can appoint any member of state legislative assembly to preside over its proceedings when the offices of both Speaker and Deputy Speaker fall vacant
  • When a bill is sent to the Governor after it is passed by state legislature he can:
    • Give assent
    • Decline assent / withhold
    • Return for reconsideration of state legislature
    • Pass to President
  • Promulgate ordinance when the state legislature is not in session

Judiciary Power

  • The Governor has the power to grant pardon, reprieve, respite and remission of punishment

Financial Powers

  • He/She sees that the annual financial statement (state budget) is laid before state legislature
  • Money bill can be introduced in the state legislature only with his or her prior recommendation
  • He/She can constitute a financial commission after every 5 years
  • He/She can make advances out of Contingency Funds of State to meet any unforeseen expenditure
