Attorney General

Attorney General

  • Article 76
  • Highest law officer in the country
  • Appointed by President

Qualifications:

  1. qualified to be appointed as judge of supreme court.
  2. citizen of India
  3. must have a judge of some high court for 5 years

or

  • An advocate of some high court for 10 years.

or

  • An eminent jurist in the opinion of President

Term of Office:

  1. Not fixed by constitution.
  2. Holds office during the pleasure of president
  3. doesn't contain procedure and grounds of removal in constitution

Resignation:

  1. To president
  2. Conventionally he resigns when government (council of ministers) resigns as he/she appointed on its advice.

Remuneration

  1. Not fixed by constitution.
  2. Receive such remuneration as president may determine.

Duties & function:

  1. To give advice to government of India upon such legal matters which are referred to him by president.
  2. To perform such other duties of a legal character that are assigned to him/her by president.
  3. To appear on behalf of government of India on all cases in supreme court or in which government of India is concerned.

Rights:

  • Right to speak and take part in the proceeding of both the houses of parliament or joint meeting but without Right to vote.
