Basic - Fundamental Rights

Fundamental Rights

  • written in part III of constitution
  • Article (12–35)
  • Borrowed from US constitution, In US it is called Bill of Rights.
  • In India, it is called “magna carta of India”
  • First document related to fundamental rights of citizen (magna carta)
  • Charter of Rights issued by King John of England.
  • More elaborate FR’s in Indian constitution than any other constitution including America.
  • FR’s are ideal for promoting political Democracy.
  • पहले 7 थे अब 6 है – Article था (31) Right to property

Originally it was 7 FR’s:

  • Article 31 – Right to property
  • 44th Constitutional Amendment Act 1978
  • Made legal right – Article 300 A in part XII of constitution

Features of FR’s

  1. Some are available only to citizen while others are available to all persons whether citizen, foreigner or legal person like corporation / companies.
  2. Not absolute but qualified
    • Reasonable Restrictions on them
    • Restrictions are reasonable or not are decided by courts
  3. FR’s available against arbitrary action of state. However, some of them are also available against the action of private individual.
  4. FR
    • Positive (+)
    • Negative (–) → Restrictions or limitation (नहीं किए जाते)
    • (Advantage to any section)
    • (Conferring certain privileges on the person)
    • Some are negative in character while some are positive in nature.
  5. FR’s are justiciable
    • allowing persons to move the court.
  6. FR’s are defended and granted by Supreme Court
  7. FR’s are not permanent
    • But only by constitution Amendment Act & not by ordinary law.
    • Without affecting the basic structure.
  8. FR’s can be suspended during the operation of National Emergency except Article 20 & 21
  9. Article 33 – their application to the members of arm forces, paramilitary forces, police forces, intelligence agency can be restricted.
  10. Martial law – application of FR’s can be restricted when martial law (military rule) imposed Article 34.
  11. Most of them are directly enforceable while few of them can be enforced on the basis of law (Article 35)
  12. Made only by parliament
    • so that uniformity throughout the country is maintained

Article 12 – definition of State

(FR–III)

  • Union Govt. (Central Govt.)
  • State Govt. (Legislature & Executive)
  • Local Govt. (Municipalities, Panchayati Raj)
  • Statutory Govt. (district Boards) (LIC)
  • Define the purpose term for the purpose of Part III (FRs)
  • State includes – Union Govt. (Executive & legislative organ), State Govt. (Executive & legislative organ), Local authorities (municipalities, Panchayati Raj), Statutory Authorities (like LIC, ONGC (Oil & Natural Gas Corporation))

Note: Acc. to supreme court even a private body or an agency working as an instrument of state falls within the meaning of state under (Article 12)

Article 13 → laws in-consistent with FRs –

  • All laws that are in-consistent with FRs shall be void
  • It provides for the doctrine of Judicial Review

Note: Judicial Right → Supreme Court (32), & High court (226)

Law in Article 13 includes:

  1. Permanent laws by parliament of state legislature
  2. Temporarily laws (ordinance issued by president)
  3. Statutory instrument (Rules, order, regulation or notification)

Note: Supreme court held in Kesavananda Bharti case that constitution Amendment can be challenged on the ground that it violates a FR that forms a part of Basic structure of constitution.

Article 14 → Equality before law and equal protection of law.

Equality before law

  • Absence of special privileges
  • Equal subjection of all person to the ordinary law of land
  • No person is above the law
  • It is negative concept.

Equal protection of law

  • Equality of treatment under equal circumstances
  • Similar application of same law under similar situation
  • Like treated alike
  • Positive concept.

Article 15 → Prohibition of Discrimination on certain ground

  • Only for citizens
  • State shall not discriminate against any citizen
  • Only on ground of religion, race, caste, gender or place of birth.

Assumptions →

  1. Excluded women & children
  2. Advancement of socially & educationally Backward classes
  3. Advancement of any socially & educationally Backward classes regarding their admission to educational institutions
  4. Economically weaker section.

Article 16 → Equality of opportunity in public employment

  • only for citizen
  • Equality of opportunities under state
  • only grounds only of Religion, race, caste, gender, descent (family & Background), place of birth or residence

Assumption:

  1. Parliament can make condition for certain employment on the basis of Residence.
  2. Reservation of appointments & reservation in promotion
  3. Office related to Religious Institution should belong to particular religion.
  4. Economically weaker Section.

Article 17 → (Untouchability) – (Citizen & Foreigners)

  • for all
  • It is not defined in constitution.

Article 18 → Abolition of titles except military and academic

  • Except military & academic
  • prohibits citizen from accepting titles from foreign state
  • Foreigners holding any office of profit under state cannot accept present, emolument or office under any foreign state without the consent of the president.

Article 19 → Right to Freedom (Protection of 6 rights)

  • (i) freedom speech & expression
  • (ii) Assemble peacefully & without arms
  • (iii) form association or union
  • (iv) move freely
  • (v) Reside or settle in any part of India.
  • (vi) practice any profession.
  • Available only to citizen & to shareholders of company but not to foreigner or legal person.
  • only against state action.
  • state can be impose reasonable restrictions.

Article 20 → protection in respect of conviction for offence

  • whether citizen or foreigners or legal person (available to all)
  • no ex post facto law (कोई भी कानून पीछे की तारीख से नहीं बन सकता)
  • A law cannot be applied retroactively to punish someone for an act that was not illegal at that time it was committed.
  • No person can convicted of any offence except violations of law.
  • No person subjected to penalty greater than that prescribed by law.
  • No double Jeopardy
  • no person shall punished for the same offence more than once.
  • No self-incrimination (judiciary)
  • no person accused of any offence shall be compelled to be witness against himself.

Article 21 → protection of life & personal liberty

  • (a) Due process of law (concept of American constitution) power of declaring unconstitutional any law which deprived a person of his liberty without fairness or reasonableness

(b) In England courts have no power to invalidate a law made by parliament

(c) Gopalan and maneka case

Gopalan case: Supreme court held that our constitution had embodied the English concept. (British said)

Maneka case: Supreme court held that law made by state seeks to deprived a person of his personal liberty such law shall be invalid.

Article 21(A) → Right to Education

  • free and compulsory
  • the age of 14 (of age of 6 to 14 years)
  • Added by 86th Constitutional Amendment Act 2002.
  • Unni Krishnan case → Declared in this case supreme court itself recognised right to education is a fundamental right under Article 21.

Article 22 → Protection against Arrest & detention

  • Being informed of the grounds of arrest
  • consult & to be defended by legal practitioner
  • Right to produced before a magistrate within 24 hours excluding journey time.
  • Right to Released after 24 hours unless the magistrate authorises further detention.

Article 23 → Prohibition of traffic in Human Beings & forced labour

  • Both citizen & non-citizen
  • Not only against state but also against private person.
  • Traffic in human beings includes – Buying & selling of men, women & children.
  • Devadasi
  • Slavery

Exemptions:

Compulsory service for public purpose for which it is not bound to pay. However, in imposing such service state is not permitted to discriminate on grounds only of religion, race, caste, class.

Article 24 → Prohibition of employment of child labour in factories

  • Govt. banned the employment of children below the age of 14 as domestic servant or workers in Business establishment like Hotels, dhabba’s, restaurants, shops, factories, resorts, spas, Tea shops & so on.

Article 25 → Doesn’t include right to convert another person to once own religion

Article 26 → Freedom to manage Religious Affairs

Religious Denomination Have Right to

  • establish and maintain institution
  • Right to administer its own affairs
  • Right to own and acquire movable & immovable property
  • Right to administer such property

Note: Article 26 (individual की जगह केवल religious denomination की जगह आता है)

Article 27 → Freedom from taxation for promotion of a Religion

  • the state cannot compel any citizen to pay any taxes for the promotion & maintenance of any particular religion.

Article 28 → Freedom from attending Religious Instruction

  • No Religious instruction can be provided in any educational institution wholly maintain out of state fund.

Article 29 → Protection of Interest of minorities (majority – Hinduism)

Minorities (Decided on 2 Basis)

  • Religious
  • Linguistic (Language)
  • only to citizen
  • minority having a distinct language, script & culture.
  • Right to conserve the same
  • No citizen shall be denied admission into any educational institutional maintain by state or receiving aid out of state funds on grounds only of Religion, Race, caste or language.

Article 30 → Right to minorities to establish and administer educational institutions.

  • Right to establish & administer educational institutional of their choice.
  • compensation amount fixed by state for the compulsory acquisition of any property of minority educational institution
  • In granting aid state shall not discriminate against any educational institution managed by minority

Article 32 → Right to constitutional Remedy.

  • Right to move to supreme court
