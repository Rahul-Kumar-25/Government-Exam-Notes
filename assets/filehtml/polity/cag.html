Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG)

CAG (Comptroller & Auditor General of India)

  • Article 148 (Part 5)
  • It is a constitutional authority
  • Head of Indian audit and accounts department
  • He/She is guardian of public purse and control the entire financial system of country at both centre and state level
  • In the state CAG is represented by accountant general

Salary and conditions:

  • Salary is equal to judge of supreme court
  • by parliament from consolidate fund of India (not from voting)
  • cannot be varied to his disadvantage during his term

Term:

  • 6 years or 65 years age limit reached
  • from the date on which he assume office
  • Resign → to president
  • Remove → by president on same grounds & manner as judge of supreme court (not on pleasure of president)
  • Disqualified for any further govt office after retirement

Duties:

  • To audit and report on all expenditure from consolidate fund of india, contingency fund and public account of union, states and UTs having legislative assemblies.
  • To audit and report on all trading, manufacturing, profit & loss accounts.
  • To audit & report on receipt & expenditure of all bodies and authorities substantially financed from union or state revenue, govt companies etc.

1st CAG → Shri V Narhar Rao (1948 – 1954)

present → 15th CAG Shri K Sanjay murthy (21st Nov 2024)
