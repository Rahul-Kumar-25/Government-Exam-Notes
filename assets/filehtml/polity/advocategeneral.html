Advocate General

Advocate General

  • Article 165
  • Highest law officer in the state.
  • Appointed by Governor.

Qualification:

  1. qualified to be appointed as judge of High court.
  2. citizen of India
  3. judicial office for 10 years in state

or

  • an advocate of a high court for 10 years.

Term of office:

  1. Not fixed by constitution
  2. Hold office during the pleasure of governor
  3. Doesn't contain procedure and grounds of removal in constitution

Resignation:

  1. To governor
  2. conventionally he resigns when government (council of ministers) resigns as he/she appointed on its advice.

Remuneration:

  1. Not fixed by constitution
  2. Receive such remuneration as governor may determine.

Duties and function:

  1. To give advice to government of state upon such legal matters which are referred to him by governor.
  2. To perform such other duties of a legal character that are assigned to him/her by governor.
  3. To appear on behalf of government of state in all cases in high court in which government of state is concerned.

Rights:

  • Right to speak and take part in the proceedings of house of state legislator but without right to vote.
