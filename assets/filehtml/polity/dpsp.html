Polity

Basic - DPSP

DPSP – Directive Principles of State Policy

  • Part IV
  • Article 36–51
  • From Irish constitution
  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar said DPSP – “novel feature of Indian constitution”
  • Aims → providing social & economic justice.

Article 36 → Definition of state

  • union, state, local, statutory authorities

Article 37

  • These principles are fundamental in the governance of the country and it shall be the duty of the state to apply these principles in making laws.
  • Govt. cannot be compel to implement them.
  • Not legally enforceable by the courts for their violation.

Article 38–51

Divided into three parts:

  • Social Principles
  • Gandhian
  • Liberal–Intellectual

Article 38 – Social principles

  • Promote social order and minimize inequalities in income, opportunity, status.

Article 39

  • Right to adequate means of livelihood.
  • Equitable distribution of resources.
  • Prevention of concentration of wealth.
  • Equal pay for equal work.
  • Protection of strength and health of workers.
  • Opportunities for development of children.

Added by Amendment

  • Article 39A → Free legal aid to poor (42nd Amendment Act, 1976)

Article 41

  • Right to work, education & public assistance

Article 42

  • Just & humane conditions of work and maternity relief.

Article 43

  • Wage and decent standard of life to all workers.
  • Amendment Article 43A → Secure the participation of workers in the industries.
  • (42nd Amendment Act, 1976)

Article 47

  • Raise the level of nutrition and improve public health.

Gandhian Principles

  • Article 40 → organised village panchayats.
  • Article 43 → cottage industries in rural areas (promote)
  • Amendment in Article 43B → for cooperative societies. (97th Amendment Act, 2011)
  • Article 46 → Education and economic interest of SC, ST & other weaker section.
  • Article 47 → Prohibits consumption of intoxicating drinks & drugs.
  • Article 48 → Prohibits slaughter of cows, calves and cattle.

Liberal–Intellectual

  • Article 44 → uniform civil code
  • Article 45 → Early childhood care and education of children (0–6 years)
    added by (Amendment 86th CAA 2002)
  • Article 48 → Agriculture & animal husbandry on modern & scientific lines.
  • Amendment Article 48A → Protection of environment, forest and wildlife (42nd Amendment)

Article 49

  • Protect every monument or place of historic interest.

Article 50

  • Separation of judiciary from executive.

Article 51

  • Establish international peace & security.
  • Honourable relations, laws & treaties and disputes.
