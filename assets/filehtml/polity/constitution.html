Polity

Basic - constitution

Constitution refers to set of principles which specifies the organisation & working of govt. & relationship b/w govt. & peoples in terms of their rights & duties.

Functions of Constitution :

  • it can declare and define the boundaries of political community.
  • It can declare and define nature and authority of political community.
  • Express the identity and values of national community.
  • It can declare and define the rights of citizen.
  • It can establish & regulate the political institution.
  • Divide share power b/w layers of govt.
  • Declare the official religious identity of the state.
  • Commits state to particular social, economic or development course.

Good constitution (qualities)

  • it should be clear
  • it should be rigid
  • stability to everyone
  • adaptability (acc. to the situation)
  • precise

Classification of the constitution

1. Evolve(within time prepared) and Enacted (Specially done)

Evolve :

An evolve constitution is a outcome of slow and gradual evolutionary process. Its provisions are contained in the form of convention, practices, principles & judicial decision.
ex :- British constitution

Enacted :

It is deliberately made by a constituent assembly. Its provisions are contain in the form of Book or a series of document.
for ex :- Indian constitution & American constitution

2. Written and unwritten constitution

Written :

Is one in which the provisions are incorporated in the form of book or document or series of document. It is formulated and adopted by a body known as constitution assembly.
ex :- USA, Canada, India

Unwritten :

not incorporated in the form of series of document. found in the form of convention, practices, principles, judicial decision etc.
ex :- UK, New Zealand, Israel

3. Rigid & flexible

It cannot be amended in the same way ordinary

  • Require special procedure.
  • for ex :- USA, Australia, Switzerland

Flexible can be amended in the same manner as ordinary law.

  • Does not require special procedure
  • ex :- UK, New Zealand

4. Federal & unitary

Federal :

  • Division of power b/w National & regional Govt.
  • operate in their respective jurisdiction independently
  • for ex :- USA, Switzerland, Australia, Canada.

Unitary :

  • concentration of power in the hands of National Govt.
  • it makes the regional govt. to operate as coordinate agency.
  • for ex :- UK, France, Japan, China etc.

6) Integrated & Independent Judiciary

  • Integrated :- it means one single system of courts for the whole country
  • from the supreme court to lower court all are connected.
  • These courts can apply both central & state

ex :- if someone breaks a law in any state, the same type of court can handle it anywhere in India. They all are part of same system.

  • Independent :- it means that judiciary works free from the control of the Govt. (legislature or executive)
  • Judges are not influenced by politicians or powerful people

for ex :- even if minister breaks the law, the court can punish them without any pressure.
