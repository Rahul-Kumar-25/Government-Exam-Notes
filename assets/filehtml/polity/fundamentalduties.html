Basic - Fundamental Duties

Fundamental Duties (Article 51A) – Part IV A

  • Duties for only citizens.
  • Amendment 42nd constitutional Amendment in 1976 Fundamental Duties of citizens added to constitution.
  • Borrowed from USSR
  • Recommended by Swarn Singh committee.

Duties (11)

  • To abide by the constitution and respect its ideals and institutions, the national flag, the national anthem.
  • To cherish and follow the noble ideals which inspired our National struggle for freedom.
  • To uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity & integrity of India.
  • To defend the country.
  • To promote harmony and spirit of common brotherhood among all the people of India.
  • To value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture.
  • To protect and improve the natural environment.
  • To develop the scientific temper and spirit of enquiry.
  • To safeguard the public property.
  • To strive towards excellence in all fairs of individual and collective activity.
  • Added in 86th constitutional Amendment Act 2002 to provide opportunities for education to his/her child or ward as the case may be b/w the age of 6 to 14 years.

Two case under FD’s :

  • Mohan Kumar Singhania case 1991
    Court held that Article 51A can be used to interpret laws.
  • Ramlila Maidan incident case 2012
    Court observed that common thread runs through Part III, Part IV and Part IV A of constitution while interpreting any of these provisions itself always be advisable to examine the scope of and impact of such interpretation.
