Polity

High Court

High Court

Constitutional Provisions

  • Articles 214–231 (Part VI)
  • Head of Judiciary in State
  • 25 High Courts in India

High Courts Having Jurisdiction Over More Than One State

  • Gauhati High Court – Assam, Nagaland, Mizoram, Arunachal Pradesh
  • Bombay High Court – Maharashtra, Goa, Dadra & Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu
  • Punjab & Haryana High Court – Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh

Union Territory

Only Delhi has its own High Court.

Circuit Bench

Karnataka High Court has circuit benches at Dharwad and Gulbarga.

High Courts with Permanent Benches

  • Allahabad
  • Bombay
  • Gauhati
  • Madhya Pradesh
  • Madras
  • Rajasthan

Appointment

  • Judges appointed by President
  • President consults Chief Justice of India and Governor of the State

Qualifications

  • Citizen of India
  • Not above 62 years of age
  • Judicial office for at least 10 years OR
  • Advocate of High Court for at least 10 years

Tenure

  • Retirement age – 62 years
  • Can resign to President
  • Can be removed by President
  • Can be appointed as Supreme Court judge
  • Can be transferred to another High Court by President

Removal Procedure

Same as Supreme Court (Impeachment process).

Salary & Allowances

Paid from Consolidated Fund of State.

Language

English

Oath

Administered by Governor.

After Retirement

A permanent High Court judge can practice only in Supreme Court or other High Courts (not in the same High Court where he served).
